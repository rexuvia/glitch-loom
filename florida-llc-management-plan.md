## Executive Summary

This plan outlines the complete transition from Zen Business to self-managed LLC compliance for a Florida-based single-member LLC. By taking over your own compliance, you can reduce annual costs from approximately **$499/year (Zen Business)** to **$188.75-$238.75/year** (self-managed with registered agent service), saving **$260-310/year**.

**Key Compliance Requirements:**
- **State:** Florida Annual Report (due May 1, $138.75)
- **Federal:** Schedule C on Form 1040, quarterly estimated taxes (only when profitable)
- **Local:** County business tax receipt (varies by county, typically $50-100)
- **Registered Agent:** Required Florida physical address

**Automation Potential:** High - Most deadlines, reminders, and document preparation can be automated with OpenClaw agents, making self-management as convenient as using a service.

## Florida State Requirements

### 1. Annual Report Filing

**What:** All Florida LLCs must file an annual report updating company information.

**Deadline:** January 1 - May 1 (annually)  
**Cost:** $138.75  
**Late Fee:** $400 (applies immediately after May 1)  
**Grace Period:** None - late fee kicks in May 2

**Filing Process:**
1. Visit Florida Division of Corporations (sunbiz.gov)
2. Create or log into your Sunbiz E-File Account
3. File annual report online
4. Pay by credit card or check (check must arrive by mid-September for year-end processing)

**Information Required:**
- Principal business address
- Mailing address
- Registered agent information
- Member/manager names and addresses
- Federal Employer ID Number (EIN)

**Payment Options:**
- Online: Credit card through sunbiz.gov (available until 5pm EST on May 1)
- Mail: Check (must be received earlier, typically by September for processing)

### 2. State Taxes

**Good News:** Florida has **NO state income tax** for pass-through LLCs!

**What This Means:**
- No state income tax return required
- No state franchise tax for LLCs
- Only applies if you elect corporate tax treatment (Form 8832 + Form F-1120)

**Sales Tax (if applicable):**
- Required only if selling physical products or certain services
- Register with Florida Department of Revenue
- File sales tax returns (frequency depends on volume)
- Not required for most service-based or consulting businesses

### 3. Registered Agent Requirement

**What:** A registered agent is required by Florida law to receive legal documents and official correspondence.

**Requirements:**
- Must have physical Florida address (no P.O. boxes)
- Must be available during normal business hours (9am-5pm)
- Must accept service of process on behalf of LLC

**Options:**
1. **Self-serve:** You can be your own registered agent if you have a Florida physical address
2. **Registered Agent Service:** $49-250/year
   - FloridaRegisteredAgent.com: $49/year
   - Northwest Registered Agent: ~$125/year
   - Rocket Lawyer: $250/year (or $125 with Premium)

**How to Change Registered Agent:**
- During annual report filing: Update information online (no extra fee)
- Outside annual report window: File Statement of Change of Registered Office/Agent (mail-in form, small fee)

### 4. Business Licenses & Permits

**Local Business Tax Receipt (Required):**
- **What:** Nearly all Florida counties require this (formerly called "occupational license")
- **Where:** Contact your county tax collector's office
- **Cost:** Typically $50-150/year (varies by county and business type)
- **When:** Before beginning business operations in that county
- **Renewal:** Annual

**Home-Based Business:**
- May require Home Occupation Permit
- Ensures zoning allows business activity at residence
- Check with county/city zoning office

**Industry-Specific Licenses:**
- Most service businesses: No state license required
- Professional services (accounting, legal, medical, construction, etc.): State licensing required
- Food service, health services, childcare: Additional permits required

### 5. Record-Keeping Requirements

**Documents to Maintain:**
- Operating Agreement (not filed with state, but strongly recommended)
- Articles of Organization (your formation document)
- Annual report confirmations
- Financial statements and accounting records
- Bank statements, receipts, invoices
- Correspondence with government agencies
- Meeting minutes (if formal meetings held)
- Member resolutions for major decisions

**Retention Period:**
- IRS recommends 7 years for tax records
- State documents: Indefinitely while business is active

## Federal Requirements

### 1. Tax Filing (Single-Member LLC)

**Tax Classification:**
- By default, single-member LLCs are "disregarded entities" for federal tax purposes
- Business income/loss passes through to your personal tax return
- **No separate LLC tax return required**

**What You File:**
- **Form 1040:** Your personal tax return (due April 15, 2027 for 2026 taxes)
- **Schedule C:** Profit or Loss from Business (attached to Form 1040)
- **Schedule SE:** Self-Employment Tax (Social Security and Medicare)

**When You Have No Income:**
- If LLC has zero activity and zero income: No filing required
- If LLC has expenses but no income: File Schedule C to claim loss (may offset other income)

### 2. Self-Employment Tax

**Rate:** 15.3% (12.4% Social Security + 2.9% Medicare)

**Social Security Cap:**
- Social Security tax applies to first $184,500 of net earnings (2026)
- Medicare tax (2.9%) applies to all earnings (no cap)
- Additional Medicare Tax: 0.9% on earnings over $200,000

**When It Applies:**
- Only when you have net profit from the business
- If no income yet, no self-employment tax due

### 3. Estimated Quarterly Tax Payments

**Who Must Pay:**
- Self-employed individuals who expect to owe $1,000+ in taxes
- **Not required if no income yet**

**2026 Quarterly Deadlines:**
- **Q1 (Jan 1 - Mar 31):** Due April 15, 2026
- **Q2 (Apr 1 - May 31):** Due June 15, 2026
- **Q3 (Jun 1 - Aug 31):** Due September 15, 2026
- **Q4 (Sep 1 - Dec 31):** Due January 15, 2027

**How to Pay:**
- Online: IRS Direct Pay, EFTPS, or IRS2Go app
- Mail: Form 1040-ES with check
- Calculate: Use Form 1040-ES worksheet or 90% of current year's expected tax

**Penalty for Not Paying:**
- Underpayment penalty if you owe $1,000+ and didn't pay enough quarterly
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of last year's tax (110% if high income)

### 4. Employer Identification Number (EIN)

**Maintenance:** None required!
- EIN is permanent once issued
- No annual filing or renewal
- Keep confirmation letter safe
- If lost, call IRS Business & Specialty Tax Line: 800-829-4933

**When You Need It:**
- Opening business bank account
- Filing annual report with Florida
- Hiring employees (future)
- Paying excise taxes (if applicable)

### 5. Federal Income Tax Return

**Form 1040 Deadline:** April 15 annually (for prior calendar year)

**Extensions:**
- Form 4868: Automatic 6-month extension to October 15
- Extension to file, **NOT** extension to pay
- Pay estimated taxes by April 15 to avoid penalties

### 6. Other Federal Compliance

**Currently Not Applicable (No Employees):**
- Form 941: Quarterly employment taxes
- Form W-2: Employee wage statements
- Form 940: Federal unemployment tax

**May Apply in Future:**
- Form 1099-NEC: If you pay contractors $600+
- Form 1099-MISC: If you pay rent $600+
- Form 1099-K: Payment processor reporting

### Phase 2: Choose New Registered Agent (Week 1-2)

**Option A: Be Your Own Registered Agent**
- **Cost:** $0
- **Requirements:**
  - You must have a Florida physical address (not P.O. box)
  - You must be available 9am-5pm EST weekdays
  - You must be comfortable receiving legal documents
- **Best for:** Florida residents with consistent availability

**Option B: Use Low-Cost Registered Agent Service**
- **Cost:** $49-125/year
- **Recommended:**
  - FloridaRegisteredAgent.com: $49/year
  - Northwest Registered Agent: ~$125/year (privacy benefits)
- **Best for:** Out-of-state owners or those wanting privacy

**Decision Factors:**
| Factor | Self-Serve | Paid Service |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| Cost | Free | $49-125/year |
| Privacy | Your address is public | Service address is public |
| Convenience | Must be home during business hours | Service handles all mail |
| Professional | Residential address | Commercial address |
| Mail Scanning | No (unless you add service) | Many offer scanning/forwarding |

**Action Steps:**
1. Decide which option fits your situation
2. If paid service: Sign up for new registered agent
3. Keep old agent until transition is complete (DON'T cancel Zen Business yet)

### Phase 3: Update Registered Agent (Week 2)

**Timing Strategy:**
- **Best time:** During annual report filing (Jan 1 - May 1)
  - Update registered agent info on annual report form
  - No extra filing or fees required
  - Everything updates in one step

- **Alternative:** File Statement of Change
  - Required if changing outside annual report window
  - Mail-in form to Division of Corporations
  - Small additional fee
  - Takes 2-3 weeks to process

**Recommended Approach:**
- **If annual report is due within 3 months:** Wait and update during annual report filing
- **If more than 3 months away:** File Statement of Change now

**Action Steps:**
1. Wait for annual report window (Jan 1 - May 1) OR file Statement of Change
2. File annual report with new registered agent information
3. Confirm change on Sunbiz.gov (check public record)
4. Notify new registered agent of active status

**Cancellation Process:**

2. Request cancellation
3. Confirm no automatic renewals

- Annual report window is closed and you haven't changed agent yet
- New registered agent hasn't confirmed acceptance

## Appendix: FAQ

### Q: Can I really cancel Zen Business and do this myself?

**A:** Yes! Florida LLC compliance is straightforward. The annual report takes 15 minutes to file online. With OpenClaw automation, you'll get the same (or better) reminders than Zen Business provides.

### Q: What if I miss the Florida Annual Report deadline?

**A:** A $400 late fee is applied immediately on May 2. The fee is mandatory by law. Additionally, if you don't file for multiple years, your LLC can be administratively dissolved by the state.

### Q: Do I need to file taxes if I have no income?

**A:** If your LLC has zero activity and zero expenses, no federal filing is required. If you have expenses but no income, you CAN file Schedule C to claim the loss (which may offset other income). Florida has no state income tax, so no state return either way.

### Q: Can I be my own registered agent?

**A:** Yes, if you:
1. Have a physical Florida address (not P.O. box)
2. Are available 9am-5pm EST on weekdays
3. Don't mind your home address being public record

Many people use a low-cost service ($49/year) for privacy and convenience.

### Q: What happens to my registered agent when I transition from Zen Business?

**A:** You'll update your registered agent information either:
- During annual report filing (no extra fee), OR
- By filing a Statement of Change with the state (small fee, mail-in form)

Keep Zen Business active until the change is confirmed on Sunbiz.gov.

### Q: Do I need an operating agreement?

**A:** Florida doesn't require you to FILE an operating agreement, but you should definitely HAVE one. It:
- Clarifies ownership and management structure
- Provides liability protection
- Required by most banks
- Helps with taxes and legal disputes

Zen Business may have created one for you - download it before canceling.

### Q: How do I know if I need to pay estimated quarterly taxes?

**A:** You must pay quarterly estimated taxes if you expect to owe $1,000+ in taxes for the year. General rule:
- If your net profit will be $5,000+, you'll likely owe quarterly taxes
- Safe harbor: Pay 100% of last year's tax (110% if high income)
- If no income yet, no estimated taxes required

### Q: What if I move out of Florida?

**A:** Your LLC remains a Florida LLC. You'll still:
- File Florida Annual Report
- Need a Florida registered agent (can't be self if you move)
- File taxes based on where income is earned (may need multiple state returns)

### Q: Can OpenClaw file my annual report automatically?

**A:** Not yet (requires payment info and authentication). But OpenClaw CAN:
- Remind you at multiple intervals
- Pre-fill a checklist with your info
- Guide you through the process
- Confirm filing afterward
- Store documentation

The actual filing takes 15 minutes once a year - easy enough to do manually.

### Q: What if Florida changes requirements or fees?

**A:** OpenClaw can monitor for changes:
- Automated quarterly research: "Florida LLC compliance changes 2026"
- Monitor Florida Division of Corporations news feed
- Alert you to any changes in fees, deadlines, or requirements

### Q: Should I hire a CPA or lawyer?

**A:** For basic compliance: Not necessary. The process is straightforward.

**Consider professional help if:**
- First year with significant income (CPA can teach you)
- Complex tax situation (multiple income streams, large expenses)
- Legal issues or litigation
- Considering changing tax election (S-Corp, C-Corp)
- Need strategic tax planning

**Good middle ground:** DIY compliance, but schedule one consultation with CPA in first profitable year.

### Q: What if I get audited?

**A:** Keep good records! If you:
- Maintain organized receipts and statements (OpenClaw helps)
- File honestly and accurately
- Keep records for 7 years
- Separate business and personal expenses

...then an audit is manageable. Most audits are correspondence audits (mail-based) for small businesses. If concerned, consider audit defense insurance (included in some tax software).

### Q: Can I change my mind and go back to Zen Business?

**A:** Yes, you can always sign up again if self-management doesn't work for you. But give it a try - many business owners find it empowering and actually EASIER to control their own compliance timeline.
