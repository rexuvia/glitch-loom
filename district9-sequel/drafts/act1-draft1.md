## ACT I: ARRIVAL

---

**OVER BLACK:**

The sound of static. Radio frequencies cycling. Then — a voice. Clicks and consonants that aren't human. The sound resolves into words:

CHRISTOPHER JOHNSON (V.O.)
*(radio transmission, distorted)*
This is Christopher Johnson. I left Earth seventeen years ago with a promise. I have returned. We are not soldiers. We are families. We need shelter. Please.

EXT. ORLANDO SUBURBS — NIGHT — MARCH 14, 2026

FOUND FOOTAGE. Shaky phone video. A WOMAN on a suburban porch in her bathrobe, pointing at the sky.

WOMAN
Oh my God. Oh my God, Kevin, get out here—

The camera pans up. Through palm trees and the amber haze of light pollution, LIGHTS. Moving in formation. Too slow for planes. Too many for anything.

CUT TO:

SECURITY CAMERA FOOTAGE — KISSIMMEE GAS STATION

The night clerk steps outside. Looks up. Drops his cigarette.

CUT TO:

NEWS BROADCAST — CNN

ANCHOR
—we're receiving reports of multiple unidentified objects in low Earth orbit over the southeastern United States. We want to stress that these reports are unconfirmed—

CUT TO:

ISS LIVE FEED

The curve of Earth. Florida's coastline, glittering. And above it — TWENTY-THREE SHIPS. Disc-shaped, ranging from small to enormous. Holding formation with mechanical precision.

CUT TO:

EXT. THE POND (OPPZ) — NIGHT

Behind chain-link and razor wire, PRAWNS emerge from shelters. They look up. Their clicking fills the soundtrack — a rising frequency, somewhere between song and scream.

An ELDER PRAWN falls to her knees. Her clicks translate in subtitle:

ELDER PRAWN (SUBTITLED)
They came back. They actually came back.

CUT TO:

NEWS MONTAGE — RAPID-FIRE

Markets crashing. The UN General Assembly in chaos. A PENTAGON SPOKESMAN sweating through a press conference. PROTESTERS in Times Square, signs already printed: "SEND THEM BACK." Counter-protesters: "REFUGEES WELCOME."

The President of the United States at a podium:

PRESIDENT (ON TV)
We are in communication with Commander Johnson. The United States will respond to this development with caution, compassion, and strength.

TITLE CARD:

**FIVE MONTHS LATER**

EXT. INTERSTATE 4 — ORLANDO, FLORIDA — DAY — AUGUST 2026

DOCUMENTARY FOOTAGE. A handheld camera in the passenger seat of a moving car. Outside: I-4's six lanes of heat-shimmer asphalt, palm trees, billboards. A billboard for Universal Studios: "THE ADVENTURE OF A LIFETIME." Next to it, a newer billboard: "HALCYON SOLUTIONS — BUILDING TOMORROW'S COMMUNITIES TODAY." The Halcyon logo: a stylized sunrise.

ELENA VASQUEZ (29, South African, intense and warm) drives. She speaks to the camera with the practiced ease of someone who's been doing this her whole life.

ELENA
My name is Elena Vasquez. I'm a documentary filmmaker from Cape Town. I should tell you two things before we start.

She adjusts the rearview mirror. The Orlando skyline glitters in the distance.

ELENA (CONT'D)
First: my father was Joaquín Vasquez. He was a journalist in Johannesburg who covered the original District 9 events. He was killed in the District 10 riots in 2013. He died with a camera in his hand. So I come to this story with... baggage. I'm not going to pretend otherwise.

Beat.

ELENA (CONT'D)
Second: this project was partially funded by a Halcyon Solutions Group cultural outreach grant. I accepted it because it gave me access I couldn't get otherwise. I want to be transparent about that.

She says it like it's ethical disclosure. Something in her eyes says she's still convincing herself.

ELENA (CONT'D)
I came to Orlando four months ago to make a small documentary about the Poleepkwa Processing Zone — what everyone calls "the Pond." Then twenty-three ships showed up in the sky and my small documentary became... this.

She gestures ahead. On the highway, a MILITARY CONVOY passes in the opposite direction. Humvees. An armored personnel carrier.

ELENA (CONT'D)
This is District 10.

TITLE CARD — stark white on black:

# DISTRICT 10

EXT. OPPZ ENTRANCE GATE — DAY

DOCUMENTARY FOOTAGE. Elena and her small CREW (two camera operators — TOMÁS and PRECIOUS) approach the main gate of the Orlando Poleepkwa Processing Zone. 

The gate is a checkpoint: concrete barriers, scanning equipment, HALCYON GUARDS in khaki tactical gear with body cameras and holstered sidearms. A sign reads: "OPPZ — AUTHORIZED ENTRY ONLY — HALCYON SOLUTIONS GROUP." Below it, someone has spray-painted in Poleepkwa clicks (subtitled): "WELCOME TO HELL."

A GUARD waves Elena through after checking credentials. Her Halcyon access badge: MEDIA — APPROVED.

ELENA (V.O.)
The Pond houses approximately one hundred thousand Poleepkwa — sixty thousand who were already in Florida before the fleet arrived, and forty thousand from Christopher Johnson's ships. It's managed entirely by Halcyon Solutions Group, a private contractor that rose from the ashes of Multi-National United — MNU — the company that made the original District 9 such a... memorable story.

INT./EXT. THE POND — VARIOUS — DAY

DOCUMENTARY FOOTAGE. Elena walks through the Pond. The camera captures:

A PREFAB HOUSING BLOCK — Halcyon-branded, already deteriorating. Water stains. Cracked walls. Overcrowded — PRAWNS visible through every window, sleeping in shifts.

The HALCYON COMMUNITY STORE. Shelves of basic goods at marked-up prices. A sign: "HALCYON CREDITS ONLY — NO CASH." A PRAWN WOMAN argues with a HUMAN CLERK.

PRAWN WOMAN (SUBTITLED)
Nineteen credits for cat food? It was twelve last week.

CLERK
Supply chain issues, ma'am. The hurricane season—

PRAWN WOMAN (SUBTITLED)
Don't "ma'am" me. You know what I am? I'm hungry. That's what I am.

Elena's camera catches it all. Then she crosses an invisible line — from the Halcyon-managed blocks to the SWAMP MARGIN.

Here: ORGANIC PRAWN STRUCTURES. Nothing like the prefab. These are grown, not built — bio-secretions hardened into curving walls, integrated with cypress roots and Spanish moss. They breathe with the humidity, surfaces glistening with moisture. They are alien and beautiful and alive.

PRAWN CHILDREN play in the water between structures. An ELDER teaches YOUNG PRAWNS something — clicking in rhythmic patterns that sound like music.

Elena, on camera, is visibly moved.

ELENA
You see the contrast? Halcyon builds for prawns. Prawns build for themselves. Guess which ones survive hurricane season.

---

EXT. THE ENCOUNTER ZONE — INTERNATIONAL DRIVE — DAY

DOCUMENTARY FOOTAGE. Elena exits the Pond and drives to International Drive — Orlando's tourist strip. Neon. Chain restaurants. Gift shops.

THE ENCOUNTER ZONE. A sleek, air-conditioned building with a holographic sign: "MEET REAL ALIENS! FAMILY FRIENDLY! $45/ADULT, $30/CHILD."

Inside: TOURIST FAMILIES interact with PRAWN WORKERS in a controlled, sanitized environment. The prawns wear clean jumpsuits. They perform "cultural demonstrations" — showing how they eat, how they communicate, how they build.

A TOURIST DAD puts his arm around a prawn for a photo. The prawn smiles (prawns can't actually smile — it's a learned facial approximation, a stretching of mandibles).

TOURIST KID
Mommy, can it do a trick?

PRAWN WORKER
*(coached enthusiasm)*
I can show you how we eat! Would you like that?

The kid screams with delight. The prawn opens a can of cat food. Scoops it with a claw. Eats — the shredding, absorbing motion of prawn feeding, which is objectively revolting up close. The tourists squeal.

TOURIST DAD
That is so cool.

Elena interviews a PRAWN WORKER outside, after his shift. He's still in his jumpsuit. He's been coached — says the right things.

PRAWN WORKER
The Encounter Zone provides meaningful cross-cultural engagement—

ELENA
Do you like working here?

A beat. His coached expression falters. His eyes — compound, alien, but unmistakably exhausted — look past Elena's camera at the parking lot full of minivans.

PRAWN WORKER
I like eating.

---

INT. HALCYON SOLUTIONS HQ — CONFERENCE ROOM — DAY

DOCUMENTARY FOOTAGE. A glass tower on International Drive, twenty stories of corporate gleam. Inside: a conference room with a view of Orlando's skyline. In the distance, barely visible through haze: the spire of Cinderella Castle.

GABRIEL (30, Earth-born prawn, wearing a polo shirt and Halcyon ID badge) sits next to MARCUS BRIDWELL (48, ex-CIA, khakis and calm authority). Gabriel introduces Elena.

GABRIEL
Elena, this is Director Bridwell. He oversees all Florida operations.

Marcus shakes Elena's hand. Warm grip. Easy smile. The kind of man who makes you feel heard.

MARCUS
Elena. Welcome. We're thrilled to support your project.

ELENA
Thank you for the access. And the funding.

She says it neutrally. Marcus doesn't blink.

MARCUS
Halcyon believes in transparency. What we do in the Pond — the integration work, the labor placement, the community development — it's important that the public sees it. Warts and all.

ELENA
Some would say the warts are the story.

MARCUS
*(smiling)*
Some would. We're confident the full picture is more nuanced.

Elena shifts to the fleet.

ELENA
How would you characterize the negotiations with Commander Johnson?

MARCUS
Productive. Proceeding in good faith. Commander Johnson is a reasonable partner. We share the same goal — a safe, integrated future for the Poleepkwa population.

Gabriel nods along. His clicking — involuntary, barely audible — suggests something else.

ELENA
And the fleet's weapons?

Marcus doesn't hesitate.

MARCUS
Commander Johnson has been forthright about his fleet's defensive capabilities. We take that seriously. But we're focused on dialogue, not deterrence.

After the interview, in the elevator, Elena's crew camera catches Gabriel alone for a moment. He removes his Halcyon ID badge, looks at it, puts it back on. The gesture is tiny. It says everything.

EXT./INT. THE POND — ZARA'S HOUSING BLOCK — DAY

DOCUMENTARY FOOTAGE. Elena interviews ZARA (16, Earth-born prawn, electric with intelligence and anger).

Zara's space: a shared room in a prefab block, decorated with human pop culture detritus — magazine pages taped to walls, a cracked phone, a ring light made from salvaged LED strips.

ZARA (SUBTITLED)
Twenty-two million followers. That's more than the governor. You know what the governor has that I don't? Everything else.

Elena laughs.

ELENA
Tell me about the fleet.

ZARA (SUBTITLED)
*(switching to English, fluent but accented)*
Forty thousand more of us. When they can't figure out what to do with the hundred thousand already here. It's like — you know when there's a fire and someone keeps bringing furniture INTO the building? "Here's a couch! You're welcome!"

ELENA
You're not happy they're here.

ZARA
I'm not happy about anything. That's my brand.

*(beat, more serious)*
Look, I don't know those people. I've never seen the homeworld. Never been off this planet. I was born in Johannesburg. I grew up in a camp. I speak English better than I speak Poleepkwa, which REALLY pisses off the old-timers. I'm Terrapkwa — Earth-born. We're not the same as them.

ELENA
You're the same species.

ZARA
Are you the same as a French person? You're the same species.

Elena concedes the point.

ZARA (CONT'D)
And don't get me started on Gabriel. That *tesh'kora*—

ELENA
What does that mean?

ZARA
*(grinning)*
Something very rude about water. He's Halcyon's pet. Wears their shirt. Speaks their words. Helps them count us and chip us and tell us to be grateful. He's not one of us.

She pauses. Something honest crosses her face.

ZARA (CONT'D)
The funny thing is, I do the same thing, right? Speak English. Use a human phone. Want human rights. So what's the difference between me and Gabriel?

*(beat)*

The difference is I don't pretend I'm not angry about it.

INT. PRAWN DWELLING — SWAMP MARGIN — DAWN

NOT DOCUMENTARY. The camera shifts — no longer handheld, no longer Elena's. Cinematic. A gentle push-in on an ORGANIC PRAWN DWELLING in the swamp margin. Walls that breathe. Water beading on surfaces that aren't quite metal, aren't quite shell.

A PRAWN wakes. He moves differently from the prawns we've seen — something in the rhythm is off. Slightly too deliberate. Like someone who learned a language fluently but still thinks in their mother tongue.

This is WIKUS VAN DE MERWE. We don't know that yet.

He prepares food: cat food mixed with diced citrus (stolen from someone's tree — a small crime of survival). His claws work the knife with practiced alien grace, but occasionally he'll grip something the way a human would — palm, not claw. Seventeen years of habit, never quite erased.

He scratches his forearm. A piece of exoskeleton flakes off, revealing new growth underneath — paler, smoother. He examines it with the detachment of long familiarity. He flicks it into the water, where a small fish eats it.

He walks to DR. JAMES OKAFOR'S CLINIC — a Halcyon-sanctioned prefab structure with hand-painted signs in English and Poleepkwa. Inside: overcrowded, under-resourced, but clean. OKAFOR (42, Nigerian-American, exhausted and warm) is treating a PRAWN PATIENT with a respiratory issue. He uses human and prawn instruments in tandem.

The prawn — Wikus — waits. When Okafor is free:

WIKUS
*(hushed English, South African accent distorted by prawn vocal apparatus)*
Morning, James.

OKAFOR
Morning, W. How's the molt?

WIKUS
Itchy. Always itchy. Like the worst sunburn you've ever had, except it never ends and also you're a giant prawn.

Okafor smiles — a familiar exchange.

OKAFOR
I have good news and I have news. Which do you want?

WIKUS
I want coffee, but my body rejects caffeine now, so give me the good news.

OKAFOR
The good news is your bloodwork looks stable.

WIKUS
And the news?

OKAFOR
*(beat)*
He's back. Christopher Johnson actually came back.

Wikus goes very still. The clicking — involuntary, emotional — fills the silence.

WIKUS
I know. I've been trying not to think about it.

OKAFOR
It's been five months, W. You can't not-think-about-it forever.

WIKUS
*(long pause)*
Watch me.

He leaves. From the door, without turning:

WIKUS (CONT'D)
He actually came back.

OKAFOR
He actually came back.

Wikus steps into the Florida morning — humid air, spanish moss, the distant thrum of I-4 traffic, and above it all, invisible in daylight but always there: the fleet.

EXT. THE POND — MEDIA ZONE — DAY

DOCUMENTARY/NEWS FOOTAGE. A cleared field in the Pond has been set up as a media zone — podium, flags, cameras. COMMAND ONE is visible in the daytime sky as a faint metallic glint.

CHRISTOPHER JOHNSON stands at the podium. He is older — seventeen years of war, politics, and loss etched into his exoskeleton. He moves with the weight of authority he never wanted.

Behind him: GOVERNOR PATRICIA HARTLEY (55, polished, calculated) and MARCUS BRIDWELL. To Christopher's left: KATO (20, military-rigid, scanning the crowd of human journalists like threat assessment).

Christopher speaks in careful, accented English.

CHRISTOPHER
I stand before you not as a conqueror. Not as a curiosity. I stand as a father who could not keep his first promise — to save our world — and so I have come to keep his second — to find a home for his people.

He activates a HOLOGRAPHIC DISPLAY — Poleepkwa technology, a shimmering three-dimensional image that elicits gasps from the press corps. The image: the HOMEWORLD. Oceans. Organic cities that dwarf human architecture. A sky of amber and violet.

CHRISTOPHER (CONT'D)
This is — was — Kep'mara. Our world.

The image shifts. The same planet, devastated. Cities in ruin. Oceans dark. The amber sky choked with ash.

CHRISTOPHER (CONT'D)
Civil war. Resource collapse. A story not unlike your own history, repeated at a scale you can imagine but perhaps do not wish to. Forty thousand of us survived. We are what remains.

He looks directly into the cameras.

CHRISTOPHER (CONT'D)
I formally request permanent settlement rights for my people. We bring technology, knowledge, and a willingness to build — together. Our ships are peaceful.

A pause.

CHRISTOPHER (CONT'D)
They are also capable of defending themselves. I trust we will not need to demonstrate this.

In the press corps, pens scribble. Cameras flash. Elena, in the crowd, captures it all.

Behind Christopher, KATO clicks something to a fellow FLEET PRAWN. The human translators don't catch it. GABRIEL, standing off to the side with a Halcyon earpiece, does catch it. His mandibles tighten. He says nothing.

Governor Hartley steps to the microphone.

HARTLEY
Florida welcomes this dialogue. We will work with Commander Johnson and with our federal partners to ensure a safe, orderly, and lawful process.

Every word tested in focus groups. Every syllable a hedge.

EXT. THE POND — SWAMP MARGIN — NIGHT

NOT DOCUMENTARY. Wikus makes his way through the swamp. BIOLUMINESCENT ALGAE lights his path — blue-green, pulsing softly. The water is warm. The sounds: frogs, insects, and underneath them, a low vibration that isn't terrestrial. The swamp around prawn settlements has changed. Trees grow faster, denser. The air smells different — not bad, but *other*.

An ALLIGATOR surfaces ten feet from Wikus. Enormous, ancient. Its scales have a faint iridescent sheen — something in the water has affected it. Wikus and the alligator regard each other. A long beat. The alligator submerges. Mutual respect between apex predators.

Wikus reaches the TESH'VA — the "free water" settlement. Organic structures built into and around cypress trees, forming a village that looks grown rather than made. Unregistered prawns — no bio-chips, no Halcyon oversight.

A TESH'VA LEADER meets him. They speak in Poleepkwa (subtitled).

TESH'VA LEADER
The sweeps are increasing. Three this month.

WIKUS (SUBTITLED)
I know. Stay deeper in the swamp. I'll bring supplies Thursday.

TESH'VA LEADER
And the fleet? What does it mean for us?

WIKUS (SUBTITLED)
I don't know yet.

He pauses at the settlement's edge. Looks up through the canopy. Between cypress branches, the lights of COMMAND ONE arc across the sky like a slow-moving star.

He watches it the way a shipwrecked man watches a distant sail.

EXT. THE POND — FLEET SECTION BOUNDARY — DAY

NOT DOCUMENTARY. KATO walks through the Earth-prawn section with two FLEET SOLDIERS. He surveys the environment with barely concealed disgust. Cat food containers. Human graffiti. A prawn wearing a Miami Heat jersey.

He stops at a group of EARTH PRAWNS speaking English among themselves.

KATO (SUBTITLED)
You've forgotten what you are.

The prawns look at him. An EARTH PRAWN — older, scarred, unimpressed — responds in Clickish (the pidgin):

EARTH PRAWN (SUBTITLED)
We remember fine. We also remember how to survive without a fleet to hide behind.

Kato SLAPS the cat food can from the prawn's hand. It clatters across concrete. The Earth prawn stands. Fleet soldiers step forward. Earth prawns gather behind their man. The air crackles.

ZARA pushes through the crowd, phone up, filming.

ZARA
*(in English, loud)*
Hey! Homeworld! You want to start a war, start it with me. I've got twenty-two million witnesses.

She holds up her phone, screen facing Kato. The live viewer count climbing.

Kato stares at her. Confused. Angry. And — despite everything — arrested by the sheer audacity. She's a head shorter than him, half his weight, armed with nothing but a cracked phone and absolute conviction.

KATO
*(broken English)*
You are... not afraid?

ZARA
I'm terrified. But scared looks a lot like brave when you're live-streaming.

A beat. Kato turns and leaves. His soldiers follow.

The crowd exhales. Zara's hands are shaking. She lowers the phone. Looks at the viewer count: 847,000 and climbing.

ZARA (CONT'D)
*(to the camera, quiet)*
Hey guys. So that just happened.

INT. ORLANDO INTERNATIONAL AIRPORT — CONFERENCE ROOM — DAY

NOT DOCUMENTARY. A windowless room repurposed for negotiation. CHRISTOPHER sits across from MARCUS, GOVERNOR HARTLEY, a DOD REPRESENTATIVE, and two STATE DEPARTMENT OFFICIALS. GABRIEL translates, seated between the sides.

Marcus slides a document across the table.

MARCUS
Commander Johnson, Halcyon's integration proposal. Housing allocation, labor placement, community services. And a joint research framework for the — for your synthesis technology. Shared access. Mutual benefit.

Christopher opens the document. Reads. His clicking increases in frequency — anyone who knows prawns recognizes agitation.

CHRISTOPHER
You want the Synthesis Engine.

MARCUS
We want partnership.

CHRISTOPHER
Partnership. The last human organization that wanted partnership with my people carved them open in laboratories to study their weapons systems.

Silence. Hartley shifts in her seat.

MARCUS
MNU was a different organization—

CHRISTOPHER
MNU was your parent company. Halcyon inherited their contracts, their databases, their personnel files. Some of your employees worked for MNU. Some of them worked in those laboratories.

Gabriel translates a Poleepkwa phrase Christopher adds — but softens it. Christopher's head turns toward Gabriel. He noticed.

MARCUS
*(smile unchanged)*
Commander Johnson, cooperation is always preferable to the alternative.

Christopher stands.

CHRISTOPHER
I have heard those words before. In Johannesburg. From men who are now in prison.

*(to Gabriel, in Poleepkwa, subtitled)*
And you — you changed my words. You will remember whose side you are on, or you will learn what it means to have no side at all.

Gabriel flinches. Christopher exits.

In the hallway, KATO is waiting.

KATO (SUBTITLED)
Let me handle this, Father.

CHRISTOPHER (SUBTITLED)
No.

KATO (SUBTITLED)
How long do you negotiate before you admit they're not listening?

CHRISTOPHER (SUBTITLED)
As long as it takes.

KATO (SUBTITLED)
It took seventeen years last time.

Christopher doesn't answer. He walks. Kato watches him go — love and frustration in a face that most humans can't read. We're learning to read it.

EXT. THE POND — OUTER FENCE — NIGHT

NOT DOCUMENTARY. The quietest scene in the film.

Wikus walks to the outer fence. Chain-link and razor wire, the Orlando skyline beyond. Traffic on the 528. The distant glow of theme parks.

He carries a SMALL METAL FLOWER — exquisitely crafted from wire, scrap aluminum, and a piece of iridescent exoskeleton shed. It's beautiful. The work of years of practice with alien hands.

He places it in the chain-link. Points it south.

He stands there.

WIKUS (V.O.)
*(the documentary talking-head style — addressing an imaginary camera)*
You think you'd stop missing them. Seventeen years. You'd think the missing would fade, like a bruise. It doesn't fade. You just get stronger. And the bruise stays the same size and you get bigger around it and one day you realize the bruise is the biggest part of you. The missing is who you ARE.

*(beat)*

Tania. Baby. I made you another flower. This one's better than last year's. I'm getting good at this. Only took me seventeen years and ten fookin' fingers I don't have anymore.

He looks up. Command One crosses the sky.

He clenches his prawn fists. The knuckles — chitin over something that used to be bone — crack.

WIKUS (V.O.) (CONT'D)
Christopher came back. He actually came back. And all I can think is — maybe. Maybe there's a way back to you.

*(long pause)*

Or maybe there isn't. And this is just another flower in a fence.

He turns and walks back into the Pond. Behind him, the flower glints in the chain-link. South.
