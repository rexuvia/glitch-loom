## ACT III: LANDFALL

---

EXT. THE POND — VARIOUS — CONTINUOUS

The hurricane and the assault arrive together. CARMEN'S EYEWALL meets the Pond at the same moment Halcyon's armored vehicles breach the main gate. The result is PANDEMONIUM.

Rain like a wall. Wind tearing sheet metal from prefab roofs — the pieces become projectiles. Floodwater rising through streets that were never designed for drainage. And through all of it: HALCYON CONTRACTORS in storm gear, night vision, armored vehicles with spotlights cutting through the deluge.

Their target is specific: SUBJECT ZERO. Wikus van de Merwe. They have his location from Gabriel's intel. But the Pond is chaos — structures collapsing, prawns fleeing, the geography transforming in real-time as floodwater reroutes streets into rivers.

National Guard units establish a perimeter — Hartley authorized "hurricane security." The Guard soldiers look confused: they expected to be stacking sandbags, not supporting a tactical operation.

NATIONAL GUARD SERGEANT
*(into radio)*
Command, we've got Halcyon contractors engaging — is this a security operation or are we—

RADIO
Provide perimeter support. Halcyon has operational authority.

The sergeant looks at the Pond: prawns in the floodwater, civilians, children. This doesn't look like security.

INT./EXT. THE POND — PRAWN STRUCTURES — CONTINUOUS

Inside the organic structures: packed with sheltering prawns. The walls flex in the wind but HOLD. Water seeps through designed channels — the structures breathe with the storm, absorb it, redirect it. They were built for this.

WIKUS moves through the chaos. He knows the Pond intimately — seventeen years of walking every path, every swamp trail, every shortcut. He shouts in both English and Poleepkwa:

WIKUS
*(English)*
Get to the organic structures! The prefab isn't going to hold!
*(Poleepkwa, subtitled)*
The swamp shelters — take the children to the swamp shelters!

He hauls a PRAWN FAMILY from a collapsing prefab block, their possessions scattering into the flood. A PRAWN CHILD clings to his leg. He picks her up without breaking stride.

ELENA is there. Camera-less — her equipment is in her hotel, inaccessible. She's just a person in a hurricane.

ELENA
Wikus! They're looking for you — Halcyon, they know—

WIKUS
I know. Help me get these people to safety first.

ELENA
But—

WIKUS
FIRST.

She helps. They work together — hauling children through waist-deep floodwater, directing families to the swamp-margin structures, the ones that will survive the night. The camera is somewhere else. The story is here, but Elena is not filming it.

DARK HUMOR: A prawn family's dwelling has a human couch inside — salvaged from a foreclosed home. The couch is floating in the floodwater. PRAWN CHILDREN are sitting on it, riding it like a boat through the flooded street.

WIKUS
*(to Elena)*
That's actually brilliant.

ELENA
It's a couch.

WIKUS
It's a BOAT now. Adapt and survive, baby. First law of the Pond.

EXT. THE POND — FLEET SECTION — CONTINUOUS

KATO hears the gunfire through the storm. He sees the Halcyon vehicles. He sees his people running.

He activates the fleet communication link.

KATO (SUBTITLED)
Command One, this is Kato. Humans are assaulting the settlement. Armed contractors. They're firing on civilians. I am requesting weapons authorization and shuttle support.

A beat. Then:

COMMAND ONE (SUBTITLED)
Authorization granted. Shuttles launching.

In orbit, aboard COMMAND ONE: three ARMED SHUTTLES detach from the flagship. They descend — through the hurricane, through the cloud bands, through the violence of a Category 4 storm. Alien craft in an earthly apocalypse. They glow — energy systems humming — as they drop through the clouds. Lightning arcs around them. They don't flinch.

From the ground, the sight is TERRIFYING and SPECTACULAR. Glowing alien ships descending through a hurricane. News helicopters (the few still airborne) capture it. The footage will define a generation.

Kato arms his FLEET SOLDIERS with energy weapons from the shuttle stores. The rifles charge with a high-pitched whine that cuts through the wind.

CHRISTOPHER, in the Pond, hears the shuttles. Sees the weapons. His wound is still fresh from the negotiation stress — he's not at full strength.

CHRISTOPHER
*(into communicator, in English, desperate)*
Kato — do NOT fire. If the fleet fires on humans—

KATO (SUBTITLED)
They are ALREADY firing on US, Father.

CHRISTOPHER (SUBTITLED)
And if we fire back, we become what they fear. We become the invasion. Everything I did — seventeen years of travel, of diplomacy, of begging — it means NOTHING if we fire first.

KATO (SUBTITLED)
Then what do we DO?

Christopher doesn't have an answer. Not yet.

EXT. ORLANDO — THEME PARK PERIMETER — CONTINUOUS

**THE THEME PARK SETPIECE.**

The chaos at the Pond's western boundary spills toward Walt Disney World property. A group of PRAWNS — families, children, fleeing the assault — breach the Disney perimeter fence (weakened by the hurricane). They scatter into the EMPTY PARK. Evacuated. Rides shut down. The Happiest Place on Earth: dark, rain-lashed, abandoned.

HALCYON CONTRACTORS follow, pursuing through the park.

The surreal imagery: PRAWN FAMILIES hiding in shuttered gift shops on Main Street U.S.A. A HALCYON DRONE navigating through the wreckage of a collapsed ride structure. An ARMORED VEHICLE rolling past the It's a Small World facade, its speakers still playing the song on emergency loop, distorted by water damage.

A PRAWN CHILD — maybe seven, Earth-born, wearing a Mickey Mouse t-shirt she got from somewhere — stands in the PLAZA IN FRONT OF CINDERELLA CASTLE. Rain hammering. Wind pulling her hair (prawns don't have hair — she's wearing a human wig, a child's dress-up thing, and it's being torn from her head by the wind).

She looks up at the castle. The fantasy spire against a real hurricane sky. Animatronic aliens were entertainment. She is real. This was supposed to be the happiest place on Earth.

A spotlight finds her. A Halcyon drone. She freezes.

WIKUS appears. Running through the flood, through the abandoned park, to this child. He scoops her up. She buries her face in his exoskeleton.

WIKUS
*(in Poleepkwa, subtitled, soft)*
I've got you. I've got you. Close your eyes.

He runs. Through maintenance access — the TUNNEL SYSTEM beneath the park. The hidden infrastructure that makes the magic work. Pipes, wires, the skeleton of fantasy. Wikus and the child in the guts of the machine.

They emerge on the far side. Other prawns are gathering there — a growing group of refugees who've found the tunnels. One of Elena's camera operators — PRECIOUS — is with them. She has her camera. She's been filming instinctively.

Precious captures the image: prawns sheltering in the maintenance tunnels of Disney World. The reality beneath the fantasy. The people behind the curtain.

EXT. THE POND — SHUTTLE PAD — CONTINUOUS

Marcus's PRIMARY OBJECTIVE: the shuttle pad on the Pond's southern edge. This is where Christopher's shuttle lands and launches. Control the pad, control access to Command One, control the Synthesis Engine.

Halcyon's TACTICAL TEAM assaults the pad in force. Christopher is there with a SMALL GUARD — fleet soldiers, but outgunned by the combination of human ballistics and numbers.

A FIREFIGHT: energy weapons vs. automatic rifles, in hurricane conditions. Energy bolts illuminate the rain in blue-white flashes. Bullets spark off prawn-made barricades. The sound is overwhelming — wind, gunfire, alien weapon discharge, and above it all, the howl of a Category 4 storm.

CHRISTOPHER takes a round. Lower thorax — a ballistic bullet that penetrates between exoskeleton plates. He falls. His guard closes around him, returning fire.

Marcus watches from a command vehicle.

MARCUS
*(into radio)*
Johnson is down. Secure the shuttle pad. Contact Command One — offer them their commander's life for the Synthesis Engine.

EXT. THE POND — ROOFTOP — CONTINUOUS

ZARA on the roof of an organic structure. The structure sways but holds. The wind threatens to tear her away — she's clipped in with improvised harness.

She has the FLEET COMMUNICATION DEVICE. She activates it. Not her usual social media — EVERYTHING. The device hijacks broadcast frequencies, satellite feeds, emergency channels, social media platforms simultaneously. Kato's technology, adapted for the biggest broadcast in history.

Her signal punches through the hurricane.

The world sees:

A sixteen-year-old prawn on a rooftop in a Category 4 storm. Behind her: the Pond in chaos — fire, floodwater, Halcyon vehicles, alien shuttles. The Disney skyline, dark, in the distance.

ZARA
*(in English, shouting over the wind)*
My name is Zara. I was born in Johannesburg. I grew up in Florida. I've never been to space.

She holds up her phone — footage playing.

ZARA (CONT'D)
This is what Halcyon Solutions Group did to us. A sterilization program. Administered through our food supply. They've been sterilizing us for two years.

The data appears on screen — Okafor's documentation. Medical records. Internal Halcyon memos.

ZARA (CONT'D)
And this—

Elena's footage plays. The raid. The forced molting. The elder's death. The Encounter Zone. The Halcyon store. Gabriel's face, translating through a megaphone. And then:

ELENA (V.O. — recorded)
My name is Elena Vasquez. This film was funded by Halcyon Solutions. I took their money for access. I was wrong. They used me. Here is what they didn't want you to see.

The footage continues: the full sterilization evidence. The Pond conditions. Everything.

Then: the assault footage. Live. Happening NOW. Halcyon contractors firing into prawn crowds during a hurricane. The shuttle pad firefight. Christopher falling. Fleet shuttles descending with weapons hot.

ZARA
*(to the camera, to the world)*
You've been watching us like a show. Like a documentary. Like content. Now watch this. They're killing us while the storm kills us and NOBODY IS COMING.

*(beat, quieter, rain streaming down her face)*

If you're watching — and I know you're watching, because that's what you do, you watch — then watch close. Because this is what it looks like. This is what it actually looks like when you decide some people aren't people.

She turns the camera to show the Pond panorama: destruction, chaos, beauty. The organic structures glowing faintly in the storm. The fleet shuttles hovering. The human vehicles below. Two worlds at war in a hurricane.

Viewer count, rolling across the bottom of every screen on Earth: 200 million. 300 million. 500 million. Still climbing.

INT. EMERGENCY OPERATIONS CENTER — TALLAHASSEE

Governor Hartley watches Zara's broadcast. Her advisors are white-faced. The sterilization evidence. The Halcyon grant to Elena. The live assault.

AIDE
Governor, this is going global. Every network—

HARTLEY
I can see that.

She watches. The prawn child at Cinderella Castle. Christopher falling. The forced molting footage replayed.

AIDE
You authorized the security—

HARTLEY
I authorized hurricane security. Not... this.

AIDE
That's a distinction that won't hold.

Hartley stares at the screen. The math is changing in real-time. Every second of broadcast shifts the calculus.

She picks up the phone. Direct line to the National Guard commander on the ground.

HARTLEY
General Banks. Stand down all Guard units. Immediately. Provide humanitarian relief only. And General? Arrest any Halcyon personnel engaged in offensive operations. The state of Florida does not authorize this.

She goes live. Her own broadcast, cutting into the feeds alongside Zara's.

HARTLEY (CONT'D)
I have ordered all Florida National Guard forces to stand down and redirect to hurricane relief and civilian protection — human AND Poleepkwa. I was not informed of the scope of Halcyon Solutions' operations, and I am ordering an immediate investigation.

Every word chosen. Every pause calculated. She's covering herself AND doing the right thing. The first genuine moment of her career is also her most politically strategic.

That ambiguity is the point.

EXT. THE POND — SHUTTLE PAD — CONTINUOUS

His Halcyon contractors aren't National Guard. They answer to corporate.

MARCUS
*(into radio)*
Continue operations. All teams, Halcyon authority supersedes state orders. Secure the pad. Get me that shuttle.

He contacts Command One directly, on a frequency his techs have isolated:

MARCUS (CONT'D)

KATO (O.S.)
*(in English, cold)*

MARCUS
I want everyone to survive tonight.

KATO (O.S.)
So do I.

MARCUS
If those shuttles fire, this becomes an act of war. The US military will respond. There are carriers in the Atlantic—

KATO (O.S.)
We have twenty-three ships in orbit. Your carriers are boats.

The standoff crystallizes. Halcyon on the ground, fleet in the air, the hurricane tearing at everything, and between them: Christopher bleeding on the tarmac.

EXT. THE POND — STREETS/FLOODWATER — CONTINUOUS

WIKUS hears the standoff. Word travels fast in the Pond — clicking carries through water and wind. He hands the prawn child to another sheltering family.

WIKUS
*(to Elena, who has caught up)*
The shuttle pad. Christopher's down. Kato's going to start a war.

ELENA
What are you going to do?

WIKUS
Something stupid.

He moves through the flooded streets. Past the ruins of the prefab blocks. Past the organic structures that held. Past the body of a prawn lying in the water — a casualty, unmarked, unknown. Past a Halcyon contractor sitting in floodwater, holding a broken ankle, crying. Wikus pauses. Looks at the contractor. The contractor looks at him — terror.

WIKUS (CONT'D)
*(in English)*
Medics are that way. Can you walk?

CONTRACTOR
*(through tears)*
I — what?

WIKUS
I said CAN YOU WALK.

The contractor nods. Wikus points. Moves on.

Elena, following, doesn't comment. She doesn't need to.

EXT. THE POND — SHUTTLE PAD — CONTINUOUS

The shuttle pad. A cleared area, now ankle-deep in floodwater. Halcyon contractors on three sides, weapons up. Christopher on the ground, fleet guards around him, returning fire sporadically. Above: Kato's armed shuttles, energy weapons glowing in the rain.

Marcus in his command vehicle. On the global broadcast (via Zara, via every screen on Earth), this scene is live. 800 million viewers and climbing.

WIKUS walks out of the storm.

He walks through the floodwater, between the lines. Between the guns. One prawn, unarmed, in a hurricane. He passes the Halcyon line — a contractor raises a rifle. Wikus doesn't stop. Doesn't look. Keeps walking. The contractor doesn't fire. Not because of mercy — because of the camera. Because of 800 million eyes.

Wikus reaches the center of the shuttle pad. He stops. He's equidistant from Halcyon's guns and the fleet's weapons. Rain hammering his exoskeleton. Wind pulling at him. He plants his feet.

He looks at Marcus's command vehicle. He looks up at Kato's shuttles. He looks at the cameras — Zara's broadcast, Precious's camera (live-linked), drone feeds, satellite feeds. The world is watching.

WIKUS
*(in English, loud enough to carry, broadcast globally)*
My name is Wikus van de Merwe.

The name lands. Everyone who knows the story — and after the original documentary, that's most of the world — recognizes it. On news desks across the planet, producers scramble.

WIKUS (CONT'D)
I am — was — a white South African man. I worked a desk job. I had a wife named Tania who is better than I ever deserved. Seventeen years ago, I became something else. Not by choice. By the universe's sick sense of humor.

He turns, slowly, addressing all sides.

WIKUS (CONT'D)
For seventeen years I tried to go back. To be what I was. And tonight — in a hurricane, on the worst night of my life, and believe me when I tell you I have had some SHIT nights — tonight I want to tell you something I should have understood a long time ago.

He holds up his prawn hands. The hands that make metal flowers. The hands that held a prawn child at Cinderella Castle. The hands that directed the contractor to a medic.

WIKUS (CONT'D)
I am not what I was. I'm what I am. And what I am is standing here, between two groups of terrified beings who need to stop fookin' shooting at each other.

He turns to Marcus — speaking to the command vehicle, knowing it's also speaking to the world.

WIKUS (CONT'D)
I know you. Not you specifically — your TYPE. I WAS your type. I worked for your company, before it changed its name and its logo and NOTHING ELSE. I evicted people from their homes with a smile and a clipboard and I told myself it was just a job. It's never just a job. It's always a choice.

He turns to the sky — to Kato's shuttles.

WIKUS (CONT'D)
*(in Poleepkwa, subtitled)*
Kato. I knew your father when he was desperate and afraid, and I was cruel to him. I was cruel because I was afraid. Fear makes us cruel. Every terrible thing humans do — every terrible thing WE do — starts with fear.

*(beat)*

Don't let fear make you what they expect you to be.

He turns back. To the cameras. To 800 million people.

WIKUS (CONT'D)
*(in English)*
You can shoot me. I mean it — any side. I'm one prawn. It doesn't change anything. Except everyone's watching. And you'll all have to live with what you do next.

*(long beat, the hurricane roaring)*

So. Please. Just... stop.

Silence. Or what passes for silence in a Category 4 hurricane — which is still DEAFENING, but the guns have stopped.

Kato, aboard the lead shuttle, stares at the feed. At Wikus. At his father bleeding on the ground. At Zara's broadcast counter: 1.2 billion viewers.

Zara's voice, in his earpiece, quiet:

ZARA (O.S.)
*(in Poleepkwa, subtitled)*
Not with fists, Kato. Not tonight.

The shuttle's energy weapons dim. The glow fades. One shuttle. Then the second. Then the third.

On the ground, Marcus's command vehicle. Marcus watches the shuttle weapons power down. He watches the viewer count. He does the math. The math that has governed his entire life.

He reaches for his sidearm. Not to surrender — he's not that man. He's calculating whether one dead prawn, one bullet, might still salvage—

A HAND on his arm. His AIDE — a young woman, her face visible on the global broadcast through the vehicle's own camera feed.

AIDE
Sir. It's over.

Marcus looks at her. At his sidearm. At the cameras. At Wikus, standing alone in the rain.

He holsters the weapon. Not because he changed. Because he calculated.

MARCUS
*(into radio, flat)*
All teams. Stand down.

The contractors lower their weapons. Slowly. Reluctantly. But they lower them.

On the tarmac, Christopher — bleeding, weak — clicks. The subtitle:

CHRISTOPHER (SUBTITLED)
I told you... you were important.

Wikus sinks to his knees in the floodwater next to Christopher. His legs have given out. Adrenaline departing.

WIKUS
Ja. Vor'thane's going to be insufferable about this.

EXT. THE POND — VARIOUS — CONTINUOUS

**THE EYE OF THE STORM.**

Hurricane Carmen's eye passes over Orlando. The wind drops. The rain stops. The clouds part. For ten, maybe fifteen minutes: SILENCE. Stars. And above them, the lights of the fleet ships, arcing across the sky.

The Pond emerges from the noise into an eerie calm. Floodwater reflects the stars. The organic structures — intact, glowing faintly with bioluminescence — stand like monuments. Around them, the wreckage of the prefab blocks. The metaphor, complete: what was built TO them has collapsed. What they built THEMSELVES has survived.

Prawns emerge from shelters. Humans — Guard soldiers, even some Halcyon contractors — emerge too. Everyone is soaked, exhausted, shellshocked.

For a few minutes, no one is a species. No one is a faction. They're just survivors.

OKAFOR arrives with a National Guard medical unit (Hartley's humanitarian pivot). He rushes to Christopher. The wound is serious — the round is lodged near a vital organ cluster. Okafor works with a FLEET MEDIC, human and prawn instruments side by side.

FLEET MEDIC (SUBTITLED)

CHRISTOPHER
*(weak, in English)*

ZARA (CONT'D)

*(in English, rough but clear)*
Enough... is enough.

She touches his shoulder — the briefest contact, the most she's given anyone in the film. Then she leaves.

Wikus sits at a console. He asks the OPERATOR to connect him to a number in South Africa.

The line connects. Rings. Picks up.

Hello?

WIKUS
Hello, baby.

TANIA (O.S.)
*(voice breaking)*
Wikus?

WIKUS
Ja. It's me. It's... ja.

TANIA (O.S.)
You sound... different.

WIKUS
I look different too. You should see my fookin' face. Actually — don't. Not yet. Maybe not—

Wikus.

WIKUS
Ja?

The flowers. Every year.

WIKUS
Every year.

TANIA (O.S.)
Are you... are you going to come home?

The question. The only question. The one he's been trying to answer for seventeen years.

WIKUS
*(long beat)*
I think I am home. I think I've been home for a while and I was too stupid to notice.

TANIA (O.S.)
*(a sound between crying and laughing — the human version of Wikus's click)*
That is the most Wikus thing you've ever said.

WIKUS
Tania. Thank you. For the flowers being enough.

TANIA (O.S.)
They were always enough. YOU were always enough.

WIKUS
*(the click — the sob-laugh, the sound that is only his)*
I love you. That didn't change. Nothing about that changed.

TANIA (O.S.)
I know, my love. I always knew.

The call holds. Neither hangs up. They breathe together across continents, across species, across everything.

EXT. THE POND — WIKUS'S DWELLING — SUNSET

Wikus descends from Command One in a shuttle. Returns to the Pond. To his dwelling in the swamp margin.

He walks through the settlement. Prawns nod to him — they know, now, who he is. The word has spread. Subject Zero. The Bridge. The man who stood between armies.

He nods back. Not a hero's welcome. Just neighbors. Just home.

He reaches his dwelling. The organic walls, breathing. The water beading on surfaces that are part-alien, part-Florida, part-something new.

From his pocket, he takes the METAL FLOWER. The one he's been carrying. The one he would have left at the fence. Pointed south. Pointed at the past.

He looks at it for a long time. Then he looks at his doorstep. The entrance to the place where he sleeps, where he eats, where he lives.

He places the flower on his own doorstep. Not pointed south. Not pointed anywhere. Just here. Just placed. Home.

He goes inside.

EXT. THE POND — AERIAL — SUNSET/DUSK

A DRONE SHOT — pulling up and away. The Pond from above.

The organic prawn structures spread into the swamp, merging with the Florida landscape. Cypress and alien architecture intertwined. Bioluminescent water channels. A hybrid ecosystem — alive, strange, new.

To the west: the theme parks. Their lights coming back on as power is restored. The castle spire, lit up, fantasy restored. Eight miles from the Pond.

The Pond has no electric lights. But it GLOWS. The bioluminescent swamp. The organic structures pulsing faintly. Fleet shuttles moving like fireflies between ground and orbit.

Two worlds. Side by side. In the same frame.

The drone rises higher. Orlando. Florida. The coast. And above it all, COMMAND ONE — crossing the sky like a slow star.

No voice-over. No title cards of resolution. No "and then Congress fixed everything."

Just the image. Two worlds. One landscape. Unresolved. Alive.
