EXT. THE POND — OKAFOR'S CLINIC — DAY

DOCUMENTARY FOOTAGE. Elena enters the clinic for an interview. OKAFOR is with patients — efficient, tired, gentle. He treats a PRAWN CHILD with a skin infection, speaking softly in pidgin Poleepkwa.

ELENA

A beat too long.

Lot of us came from Johannesburg.

ELENA
A lot of you don't speak English like you learned it at university.

Okafor intervenes, gentle but firm.

Elena backs off. But later, walking through the Pond, she and "W" end up on the same path. The camera is OFF — Elena's choice. They walk.

WIKUS
You're the filmmaker. The one Halcyon is paying.

ELENA
Funding. Not paying. There's a—

WIKUS
There's no difference.

That lands. They walk in silence.

WIKUS (CONT'D)
Why are you here? Really. Not the documentary answer. The real one.

ELENA
My father died covering prawns. I want to understand what he died for.

WIKUS
*(long pause)*
He died for the story. Everyone always dies for the story. Nobody dies for us.

He turns down a swamp path and disappears. Elena watches him go. She doesn't follow. She doesn't turn on the camera. Something in her — the journalist — is screaming. She ignores it.

EXT. THE POND — MAIN GATE AND INTERIOR — DAY

**THE HALCYON RAID.**

DOCUMENTARY/REAL-TIME — multiple camera sources: Elena's crew, surveillance feeds, Zara's phone, Halcyon body cams.

It begins at dawn. HALCYON TACTICAL TEAMS in full riot gear — helmets, body armor, armored vehicles — roll through the Pond's main gate. Officially: "a routine security sweep targeting unregistered residents in the wetland margin." The pretext: the Tesh'va.

The reality is immediately apparent. This is not routine. This is shock and awe.

Contractors kick in doors. Residents are dragged into the street. IDENTIFICATION SCANNERS wave over bio-chips — anyone without a chip is flex-cuffed and thrown into transport vehicles. An ELDERLY PRAWN is shoved to the ground and held down with a boot.

GABRIEL walks with the sweep team, wearing a Halcyon vest, translating orders through a megaphone:

GABRIEL (SUBTITLED/ENGLISH)
Please remain in your designated areas. This is a routine security action. Cooperate and you will not be harmed.

A PRAWN spits at his feet.

PRAWN RESIDENT (SUBTITLED)
*Tesh'kora.* Traitor.

Gabriel's face: nothing. His hands: shaking.

The sweep reaches the market area. A HALCYON CONTRACTOR — heavy, aggressive, running hot — uses something new: a CHEMICAL AGENT deployed from a canister. The gas hits a YOUNG PRAWN who was throwing debris at the contractors.

The effect is immediate and horrifying.

The prawn's exoskeleton begins to SHED. Not the natural, gradual molting process — this is FORCED. Sheets of chitin crack and peel. The prawn SCREAMS — a sound no human throat can produce, a buzzing shriek that vibrates in the chest. Underneath the exoskeleton: raw tissue. Pink. Wet. Glistening. The nerve endings are exposed. Every breeze, every touch is agony.

Other prawns rush to help. They try to hold the molting shell in place. It keeps peeling. The prawn convulses.

ZARA is there, filming on her phone. She's crying and filming.

ZARA
*(to her audience, in English, voice cracking)*
Are you watching? Are you watching this?

A Halcyon contractor sees her phone. Steps toward her.

ZARA (CONT'D)
Don't you fookin'—

He STOMPS on the phone. The feed cuts. The screen goes black. The viewer count at the moment of blackout: 1.4 million.

In the chaos: a PRAWN ELDER — the same one we saw teaching children in the swamp margin — is thrown to the ground. The impact. She doesn't get up. A medic, eventually, checks. Cardiac arrest. She's dead.

One prawn dead. Twelve injured. One forced-molted. Zero human casualties.

The sweep withdraws. Marcus, watching the feeds from Halcyon HQ, removes his earpiece.

MARCUS
Compile the footage. Any shots of prawn aggression — throwing debris, threatening gestures — isolate and prepare for media release.

He pauses.

MARCUS (CONT'D)
And get me a damage assessment on the nerve agent deployment. That wasn't authorized.

HALCYON AIDE
Sir, Contractor Mills—

MARCUS
I don't care who. That wasn't authorized. We need this to look proportional.

The distinction: Marcus isn't upset that a prawn was forcibly molted. He's upset it was caught on camera.

**THE REUNION.**

BIOLUMINESCENT WATER between cypress trees. The swamp breathes. Somewhere, an alligator rumbles.

Christopher arrives at a clearing. He's alone — no guards, no advisors. Just a prawn in a swamp.

A shape detaches from the shadows. WIKUS.

CHRISTOPHER (SUBTITLED)

WIKUS
*(in English)*
You took your fookin' time.

CHRISTOPHER
*(in English)*

WIKUS
I WAS a bureaucrat. In another life. Literally another life.

They move closer. Not touching — there's too much history for casual contact.

CHRISTOPHER
Tell me.

WIKUS
Every year, I leave one at the fence. Pointed south. Toward Joburg. Toward Tania. My wife.

Ex-wife? Can you be an ex-wife to someone who isn't the same species anymore? There's no legal precedent. I checked. There's no legal precedent for ANY of this.

Christopher listens. Then:

CHRISTOPHER
The medical technology aboard Command One — it's advanced beyond anything the original ship carried. I cannot guarantee results. The transformation may be permanent. But we can try.

CHRISTOPHER (CONT'D)
There is also something Vor'thane wishes to tell you. About what you are. About what the transformation means.

WIKUS
I know what it means, Christopher. It means I'm a fookin' prawn.

CHRISTOPHER
*(gently)*

CHRISTOPHER (CONT'D)
This is what you are part of. What the fluid connected you to.

Wikus stares at a world he will never see. A world destroyed before he could reach it. His claws reach out — pass through the hologram.

WIKUS
It's... it's beautiful.

CHRISTOPHER
It was.

VOR'THANE (SUBTITLED)

VOR'THANE (SUBTITLED, CONT'D)
In our oldest history — before the wars, before the ships — chosen ones underwent deliberate transformation. Not human to prawn, no. Between forms of knowing. Between modes of consciousness. The Bridge Water allows one mind to hold two truths simultaneously.

The images show: a POLEEPKWA undergoing transformation. Not the violent, traumatic process Wikus experienced — a ritualized, sacred one. But the BODY HORROR is still there: the exoskeleton reforming, the consciousness splitting and reuniting, the being emerging as something that was neither what it was before nor something entirely new.

VOR'THANE (SUBTITLED, CONT'D)
This practice was lost ten thousand years ago. The Bridge Water still existed — in the fuel, in our technology — but no one knew its original purpose.

She turns to Wikus.

VOR'THANE (SUBTITLED, CONT'D)

*(first time in English — rough, ancient, each word chosen)*

VOR'THANE (SUBTITLED)
The humans have a word for everything. "Cure." As if what you are is a disease.

WIKUS
*(quiet)*

VOR'THANE (SUBTITLED)
Then you are like every chosen one before you. Wanting to be less than what they are.

*(in English)*

He says it like he's weighing it. Like fifteen percent is not nothing.

He gets out of the pod. He doesn't say yes. He doesn't say no.

INT./EXT. VARIOUS — DOCUMENTARY SEGMENTS

ELENA tracks down TANIA VAN DE MERWE via video call. Cape Town. Late evening.

TANIA (50, tired, strong, a social worker's patience and a widow's grief) appears on screen.

TANIA
I know why you're calling.

ELENA
Mrs. van de Merwe—

TANIA
He's alive. Isn't he.

Not a question.

ELENA
I... I'm investigating the possibility that your husband—

TANIA
He leaves me flowers. Every year. Small metal ones. I've never seen him do it. But every year, on our anniversary, there's a flower.

She holds one up to the camera. A small metal rose, slightly rusty. Delicate. Made by hands that aren't hands anymore.

TANIA (CONT'D)
I don't know where he is. I don't know what he looks like now. But I know this is his work. Wikus was always... he tried so hard. At everything. Even when he was terrible at it, he tried so hard.

She looks directly at the camera.

TANIA (CONT'D)
If you find him — tell him I understand. Tell him the flowers are enough. Tell him to stop punishing himself.

Elena ends the call. She stares at the wall. She knows where Wikus is. She can't tell Tania. Not yet. Maybe not ever.

INT. GOVERNOR'S MANSION — TALLAHASSEE — DAY

Hartley on cable news. Multiple screens, multiple channels, all her. She's performing.

Afterward, in private with her AIDE:

AIDE
The prawn child from the raid — the one hit with the rubber round. She's in critical condition. Okafor's clinic is treating her, but—

Something crosses Hartley's face. Real. Unpracticed. It lasts half a second.

HARTLEY
Send flowers. And make sure there's a photographer.

The aide hesitates.

HARTLEY (CONT'D)
What? Compassion without documentation is just sentiment.

She believes this. It is the most revealing thing she's ever said.

HARTLEY (CONT'D)
And get me Marcus Bridwell. He has forty-eight hours to resolve this before I deploy the National Guard.

AIDE
Resolve it how?

HARTLEY
I don't care how. Just quietly.

INT. OKAFOR'S CLINIC — PRIVATE ROOM — DAY

Elena comes to Wikus. She tells him.

ELENA
I know who you are.

Wikus tenses. Moves toward the door. Elena steps into his path — a five-foot-six human woman blocking a seven-foot alien. It is brave, stupid, or both.

ELENA (CONT'D)
The camera's off. I swear on my father.

WIKUS
Your father's dead. Swearing on dead people is easy.

ELENA
Then I swear on your wife. Tania told me to tell you: the flowers are enough. She says to stop punishing yourself.

Wikus goes absolutely still. The clicking stops. Everything stops.

WIKUS
*(barely audible)*
You spoke to Tania?

ELENA
Yesterday.

WIKUS
Is she — how is she—

ELENA
She's strong. She works with prawns in Johannesburg. She never remarried.

The silence stretches. Then Wikus does something unexpected: he sits down in front of where a camera would be — facing the wall, as if facing a lens — and begins to talk. The DOCUMENTARY TALKING-HEAD STYLE from the original film. Muscle memory from a life he lost.

WIKUS
My name is Wikus van de Merwe. I used to be human. I used to work for Multi-National United. I had a desk and a stapler and a wife and a house in Johannesburg and I was HAPPY, you know? Boring happy. Normal happy. And then I went to work one day and got sprayed in the face with alien goo and my life ended. Not — I didn't die. My life ENDED. Everything I was. Gone.

*(beat)*

The funniest part? I used to evict them. Prawns. Knock on the door, polite as anything: "Sorry, sir, you need to sign here, we're relocating you." I did it with a smile. And then God — or whoever. God's a prawn, by the way. Has to be. Only a prawn would think this was funny — turns me INTO one. Cosmic fookin' comedy, that.

*(beat)*

You know the worst part? It's not the body. You get used to the body. It's the — you go to laugh at something, right? And the sound that comes out isn't your laugh. Seventeen years and I still don't recognize my own fookin' laugh.

Elena is crying. The camera is off. She doesn't ask to turn it on.

WIKUS (CONT'D)
She said the flowers are enough?

ELENA
She said YOU were always enough.

Wikus clicks — that sound between a sob and a laugh. The hybrid sound. Neither human nor prawn. Just Wikus.

Shelter in WHAT place? The place they built from cardboard? Or the place we built from ourselves? They're evacuating the humans. They're leaving us.

WIKUS
My name is Wikus van de Merwe. I used to be human. I've been hiding for seventeen years because I was afraid.

He looks at them — the doctor, the filmmaker, the teenager. The accidental resistance.

WIKUS (CONT'D)
I'm done being afraid.

He lays out the plan: Elena's documentary footage plus Okafor's sterilization evidence, broadcast globally through Zara's platform — boosted by the fleet communication device — timed to drop when the hurricane hits. When the whole world is watching Florida.

ELENA
That exposes you. Halcyon will come for you.

WIKUS
They've been coming for me for seventeen years. I'm tired of running.

ZARA
We could reach a hundred million people.

Or we could get killed.

I've been killed once already. Dying is overrated.

Outside the dwelling, in the shadows: GABRIEL. He heard everything. He stands very still. Then he leaves.

EXT./INT. VARIOUS — THE NIGHT BEFORE — MONTAGE

The wind picks up. The first outer bands of Hurricane Carmen. Rain begins — scattered, warm, a prelude.

**WIKUS** — in his dwelling, alone. He looks at his prawn face in standing water. The reflection: alien, familiar, his. He reaches down and touches the surface. The water ripples. The face reforms.

WIKUS
*(to himself)*
Okay.

Just: okay. Seventeen years of fighting what he is. And now: okay.

**CHRISTOPHER** — aboard Command One, looking down at Earth through a viewport. Florida's coastline. The hurricane visible from orbit — a white spiral, beautiful and terrible. Kato sleeps nearby on a ship-grown cot. In sleep, the anger is gone from his face. He looks young. Christopher covers him with a cloth. The father who couldn't save a world, watching his son sleep.

**ELENA** — in her hotel room, reviewing footage. Her father's photo on the desk. She picks up the phone. Calls Cape Town. "Mama, I think I understand now. Why Daddy never stopped filming." Her mother cries. Elena cries. Neither says what they mean: "I love you, and I'm sorry, and I might not come back."

**OKAFOR** — in a borrowed room, bourbon and ceiling and silence. He's failed. The clinic is shut down. The evidence may not be enough. He pulls out his phone and looks at a photo of himself — years ago, at a medical conference, smiling, a man who had not yet seen what he's seen.

**MARCUS** — at home in Winter Park. Hurricane shutters up. His family already evacuated. He walks through the empty house. Touches his daughters' beds. Then goes to the garage, gets in his car, and drives toward Halcyon HQ through the first bands of rain.

**GABRIEL** — in his apartment. Halcyon vest on the couch. He picks up his phone. Calls a number in the Pond — a prawn he knows. We don't hear what he says. He starts crying. Prawn tears aren't liquid — they're a low, keening vibration that makes nearby objects hum.

**VOR'THANE** — aboard Command One, alone in her chamber. She clicks in ancient patterns. The ship's organic walls PULSE in response. She's communing with the ship. Or praying. Or remembering a world that no longer exists.

**ZARA** — on the roof of her housing block. The wind pulls at her. KATO is beside her. They don't speak. They watch the clouds build — massive, dark, alive.

KATO
*(in terrible English)*
Is... good view.

ZARA
It's a hurricane.

KATO
Yes. Good hurricane view.

Zara laughs. It's genuine and surprised and exactly what the audience needs.

ZARA
You're going to be terrible at English forever, aren't you?

KATO
*(in Poleepkwa, subtitled)*
I am excellent at many things. English is not among them.

They sit together as the wind builds. Two young beings on a roof, watching the end of the world.

EXT. THE POND — DAWN — THE HURRICANE HITS

DOCUMENTARY/REAL-TIME. Carmen's first eyewall bands slam Orlando. Rain horizontal. Wind: 130 mph gusts. Trees bending to breaking. The Pond's prefab structures groan and flex — not built for this.

The prawn organic structures HOLD. They flex, absorb, reseal. Designed for chaos.

Elena and her crew are filming. Tomás and Precious are terrified. Elena is terrified. She films anyway.

Then: headlights. Through the rain. Not leaving Orlando.

ARRIVING.

National Guard Humvees. Halcyon armored trucks. Drone launchers deploying under weatherproof canopies. The vehicles push through rising floodwater toward the Pond's gates.

Elena's camera catches it: this isn't hurricane relief. This is an INVASION.

MARCUS, in a command vehicle, rain streaming down the window:

MARCUS
*(into radio)*
All teams, execute Protocol Harvest. Move now. The storm is our window.

In the Pond: GABRIEL. Still wearing his Halcyon vest. He sees the vehicles arriving. He understands, finally, completely, what he has done.

He starts running. Not toward Halcyon. Toward the Pond. Into the wind.

A HALCYON CONTRACTOR sees a prawn in a Halcyon vest running the wrong direction.

CONTRACTOR
Hey — HEY! Stop!

Gabriel doesn't stop. The contractor fires. A single round.

Gabriel staggers. Falls. Crawls. The rain soaks him. He reaches a prawn dwelling and bangs on the door.

GABRIEL (SUBTITLED)
They're coming. Get to the swamp. GET TO THE SWAMP.

The door opens. A PRAWN WOMAN sees the Halcyon vest. Spits on him.

Then sees the blood.

She pulls him inside.

GABRIEL (SUBTITLED, CONT'D)
*(fading)*
I'm sorry. I'm sorry. I'm sorry.

He dies in a prawn home. Not his apartment in Kissimmee. Not Halcyon HQ. A prawn home that he helped them find.

The prawn woman holds him. She doesn't know his human name. She doesn't know his prawn name. She holds him anyway.

END OF ACT II.
