# DISTRICT 10: REVISED STORY OUTLINE (V2)
## Incorporating Critique #1 Revisions

---

## KEY CHANGES FROM V1
1. **Act I compressed** from 15 scenes to 12 — merged Pond tour/Encounter Zone, cut redundant news montage
2. **Midpoint restructured** — Halcyon raid moved earlier; Wikus-Christopher reunion IS the midpoint
3. **Added prawn collaborator character: GABRIEL (Gab'reth)** — a prawn Halcyon translator/informant
4. **Kato commits an irreversible violent act** before his redemption
5. **Resolution dirtied up** — systemic injustice persists; only personal arcs resolve
6. **Body horror enhanced** — Synthesis Engine biology, sterilization effects shown viscerally
7. **Tania appears** — voice on the phone, and one documentary interview
8. **Elena compromised** — she accepted Halcyon grant money for access; her moral journey is harder
9. **More dark humor throughout**
10. **Theme park setpiece added** — climax spillover to Disney property perimeter

---

## NEW CHARACTER: GABRIEL (*Gab'reth*)
**Role:** Halcyon Prawn Liaison / Collaborator | **Age:** 30 | **Earth-born**

Gabriel is Halcyon's model prawn — bilingual, well-groomed (by human standards), given a human name and a Halcyon ID badge. He translates, informs, and rationalizes. He genuinely believes cooperation is the path to rights — or has convinced himself he does. He feeds information to Marcus. He is despised by Pond residents, who call him *tesh'kora* ("dry water" — a prawn obscenity meaning someone who has lost their essence).

Gabriel's arc: He reports Wikus's identity to Marcus (the betrayal that triggers Act III). When the hurricane assault begins, he realizes what he's enabled. He tries to warn the Pond. He is shot by a Halcyon contractor for his trouble — dying in the crossfire, neither fully human-allied nor fully prawn-accepted. His death is unremarked in the chaos. No one mourns the collaborator. It's one of the film's most quietly devastating moments.

## REVISED ACT I: ARRIVAL (Pages 1-32)
**12 Scenes — Tighter, meaner, funnier**

### Scene 1 — COLD OPEN: THE FLEET ARRIVES (pp. 1-3)
*[Unchanged from V1]* News/phone footage montage of fleet arrival, March 2026. Christopher's message. Title card.

### Scene 2 — ELENA'S INTRODUCTION (pp. 3-5)
DOCUMENTARY. Elena drives into Orlando, August 2026. She introduces herself AND her compromise: "This project was partially funded by a Halcyon Solutions cultural outreach grant. I want to be transparent about that." She says it like it's ethical journalism. We sense it's a rationalization. She also introduces her connection to the original D9 documentary through her parents.

### Scene 3 — THE POND: WORLD TOUR (pp. 5-9)
DOCUMENTARY. Combined Pond tour AND Encounter Zone visit into one long, dynamic sequence. Elena enters the Pond — we see the checkpoints, the bio-chips, the prefab decay, the organic prawn structures. She passes through the Halcyon store (credits-only, marked-up prices — a prawn woman argues with a human clerk about the cost of cat food). She exits the Pond and goes directly to the Encounter Zone on I-Drive — the tourist "alien experience." The TONAL WHIPLASH of going from real squalor to sanitized entertainment is the point. A human family poses with a prawn worker. The prawn's eyes — above the tourist's smile — are dead.

Dark humor beat: A tourist asks the performing prawn "Do you miss your planet?" The prawn responds in coached English: "Earth is my home now! Would you like to see me eat?" The tourist's child screams in delight. The prawn eats a can of cat food. The performance.

### Scene 4 — GABRIEL: THE MODEL PRAWN (pp. 9-10)
DOCUMENTARY. Elena interviews Gabriel at Halcyon HQ — he's her Halcyon-assigned guide. He's articulate, wears a polo shirt (mimicking Marcus), and speaks about "integration milestones" and "community uplift." He seems genuine. He's also clearly performing. Elena asks him about conditions in the Pond. Gabriel: "There's always room for improvement, but Halcyon's commitment—" Elena: "Do you live in the Pond?" Gabriel: "I have a Halcyon-provided apartment in Kissimmee." Elena doesn't need to say more.

### Scene 5 — ZARA'S WORLD (pp. 10-13)
*[Largely unchanged from V1]* Elena interviews Zara. Energy, anger, charisma. We see @PrawnLife. Zara rants about the fleet. But NEW: Zara also rants about Gabriel specifically — "That tesh'kora? He's not one of us. He's their pet." Zara's contempt for prawn collaborators is visceral and complicated — because Zara herself speaks English, uses human tech, and wants human rights. The line between integration and collaboration is the theme, and Zara is aware of the irony even as she draws the line.

### Scene 6 — WIKUS IN HIDING (pp. 13-16)
*[Unchanged from V1]* "W" and Okafor. The South African accent. Christopher's return mentioned. But NEW: We see Wikus's daily routine in more physical detail — the way his prawn body moves through the swamp, the biological reality of eating (prawns don't chew; they shred and absorb — it's not pretty), the sound of his own breathing through spiracles, not lungs. BODY HORROR lite: Wikus scratches his exoskeleton and a piece flakes off, revealing new growth underneath. He examines it with the detachment of long familiarity. To him, this is just... Tuesday.

### Scene 7 — MARCUS BRIDWELL: THE INTERVIEW (pp. 16-18)
*[Enhanced from V1]* DOCUMENTARY. Marcus's interview. But now Elena's complicity is foregrounded: Marcus mentions the Halcyon grant casually. "We're glad to support Elena's important work." Elena's face on camera — she realizes the interview is partly his PR, and she's enabled it. After the interview, she calls her mother: "Mama, I think I made a mistake taking their money." Her mother: "Your father never took anyone's money. Your father is dead."

### Scene 8 — CHRISTOPHER'S PRESS CONFERENCE (pp. 18-21)
*[Enhanced from V1]* Christopher's public address. But now with ADDED DETAIL: Kato is not just background — he interrupts. In Poleepkwa, he says something that the human translators can't quite catch. Christopher smoothly covers: "My son extends his greetings." Gabriel, translating for Halcyon, knows what Kato actually said. His face tells us it wasn't greetings.

Also: Christopher maintains the fleet's weapons as a *stated* deterrent. "Our ships are peaceful. They are also capable of defending themselves. I trust we will not need to test this." This addresses the logic gap — he's not disarming, he's deterring.

### Scene 9 — THE SWAMP AT NIGHT (pp. 21-23)
*[Enhanced from V1]* Wikus visits the Tesh'va. NEW BODY HORROR: The swamp around prawn settlements has become biologically hybrid — bioluminescent algae, yes, but also something unsettling. The water itself seems to breathe. Prawn secretions have created an ecosystem that is *alive* in a way that terrestrial swamps aren't. An alligator surfaces near Wikus — enormous, but its scales have a faint iridescent sheen. Something is changing.

Wikus touches the water. His hand (claw) tingles. The water recognizes him.

### Scene 10 — KATO AND ZARA: FIRST CLASH (pp. 23-25)
*[Unchanged from V1]* Their confrontation. Cat food knocked away. Zara's phone. "22 million witnesses."

### Scene 11 — THE NEGOTIATION BREAKS DOWN (pp. 25-29)
*[Enhanced from V1]* The negotiation scene, but now Gabriel is present as translator. He's caught between: translating honestly (which inflames both sides) or softening (which serves Halcyon). He softens. Christopher notices. After the meeting, Christopher confronts Gabriel privately in Poleepkwa: "You changed my words." Gabriel: "I adjusted them. For everyone's benefit." Christopher: "Whose benefit?" Gabriel has no answer.

The negotiation still fails. Halcyon's 72-hour ultimatum follows.

### Scene 12 — THE METAL FLOWER / HALCYON MOBILIZES (pp. 29-32)
Combined scene: Wikus at the fence with his flower (the emotional beat), intercut with Marcus in Halcyon's war room receiving the order from Margaret Yates to prepare Protocol Harvest. The juxtaposition: one being mourning what he's lost, another planning to take more.

Tropical Storm Carmen mentioned on a weather screen in the background of the Halcyon war room. Nobody's paying attention to it yet.

END OF ACT I.

## REVISED ACT II: THE STORM BUILDS (Pages 33-85)
**24 Scenes — Restructured midpoint, Halcyon raid earlier, more humor and horror**

### Scene 13 — OKAFOR'S SECRET (pp. 33-35)
*[Moved earlier]* Okafor shows Elena the sterilization evidence. But now with VISCERAL DETAIL: He shows her a prawn patient — a young female whose reproductive system has been chemically destroyed. We SEE the ultrasound-equivalent scan. The internal damage is visible and horrible. The prawn doesn't understand what's been done to her. Okafor explains it while the prawn sits there, trusting, not knowing.

Elena vomits. Okafor: "That's what I did the first time too."

Elena's Halcyon grant money burns in her mind. She funded by the people who did this.

### Scene 14 — DARK HUMOR INTERLUDE (pp. 35-37)
DOCUMENTARY. A montage of "normal life" in the Pond — the absurd dailiness of alien-refugee existence. A prawn teaching a human social worker how to pronounce Poleepkwa words (the social worker's attempts are hilariously wrong). A prawn selling bootleg cat food from a trench coat ("I got Fancy Feast, I got Meow Mix, I got the good stuff — Friskies Pâté"). Two prawns watching a Disney fireworks show from outside the Pond fence, commenting on it like film critics: "The pacing is terrible. All spectacle, no narrative." "Humans."

### Scene 15 — ELENA ON THE TRAIL (pp. 37-38)
Elena cross-references "Subject Zero" files. She's closing in on Wikus's identity. She also discovers her Halcyon grant agreement has a clause she missed: Halcyon retains "review rights" over her footage. They can legally suppress her film. She is sick.

### Scene 16 — THE HALCYON RAID (pp. 38-42)
**MOVED EARLIER — now page 38 instead of 56.** The raid plays out as before, but with additions:
- Gabriel is present — wearing a Halcyon vest, translating orders. Prawns spit at him. He looks down.
- Zara's livestream captures the violence AND Gabriel's participation. She broadcasts: "Look at this tesh'kora. Wearing their clothes. Speaking their words. Helping them hurt us."
- One prawn elder dies. A prawn child is injured — a young one, caught by a rubber bullet. Okafor rushes to help but is held back by Halcyon security.
- BODY HORROR: A Halcyon contractor uses a prawn-specific chemical weapon — a nerve agent that causes involuntary molting. A prawn in the street begins shedding his exoskeleton, screaming. The raw tissue underneath is pink, wet, vulnerable. Other prawns try to help. It's District 9-level body horror.

### Scene 17 — KATO'S RAGE (pp. 42-44)
Kato responds to the raid. But now: **HE ACTS.** He doesn't just rally soldiers — he captures a Halcyon contractor who was separated from his unit during the chaos. He beats him. Badly. Other fleet prawns pull Kato off, but the contractor is seriously injured. Christopher is horrified. The footage exists — Halcyon drones recorded it. Kato has given them exactly what they wanted: a violent prawn on camera.

Kato knows what he's done. He doesn't apologize. "They hurt us. I hurt one of them. That's not the same." Christopher: "In their eyes, it's worse. Because that's how it works."

### Scene 18 — HURRICANE CARMEN: CAT 3 (pp. 44-45)
Weather update. Four days out. Trajectory: direct hit. No evacuation plan for the Pond.

### Scene 19 — ZARA AND KATO: THE REAL CONVERSATION (pp. 45-48)
After the raid, after Kato's violence. Zara confronts him — not about the violence, but about the stupidity. "You gave them the clip they need. One prawn beating one human, and that's the story. Not the raid. Not the molting agent. You." Kato is shaken because she's right. Zara shows him the comments on her feed: the human sympathy shifting after Kato's attack video went viral. "This is how we lose."

Then: vulnerability. Kato admits he doesn't know how to fight without fighting. On the homeworld, that's all there was. Zara: "This isn't the homeworld." A pause. "Welcome to Earth. It sucks and the food is terrible and we fight with phones."

She teaches him how to use social media. He's terrible at it. First prawn meme attempt: an image of a cat food can with fleet military insignia. "This is not funny," he says. Zara: "It's a little funny." He almost laughs. Prawn almost-laughing is a strange, clicking, whole-body thing. Zara records it. It gets 50 million views.

### Scene 20 — GABRIEL'S CRISIS (pp. 48-50)
Gabriel, after the raid, sits alone in his Kissimmee apartment. It's decorated with human furniture — a couch, a TV, framed motivational posters. He's watching the raid footage on the news. He switches to Zara's feed: "tesh'kora." He flinches. He opens a drawer: a bag of cat food, hidden like contraband. He eats from it with his claws, alone, the human furniture around him, the TV showing his people being beaten.

He picks up his phone and calls Marcus: "I have something. There's a prawn in the Pond — the English speaker. I think he might be Subject Zero."

The betrayal.

### Scene 21 — ELENA CONFRONTS HER COMPLICITY (pp. 50-52)
Elena tries to void her Halcyon grant. She can't — the legal clause means they can suppress her footage AND sue her. She's trapped. She confesses to Okafor: "I took their money. I thought it was just access. I'm as bad as—" Okafor: "You're not as bad as them. But you're not clean either. None of us are. That's the point."

Elena makes a decision: she'll release the footage WITHOUT Halcyon's approval, in violation of her contract. It'll destroy her career. She calls her mother to tell her. Her mother starts crying. Elena doesn't know if it's pride or grief.

### Scene 22 — WIKUS AND CHRISTOPHER: REUNION (pp. 52-57)
**THE MIDPOINT.** Now properly centered at page 52-57. *[Plays as V1 but enhanced:]* The swamp reunion. "You took your fookin' time." Seventeen years compressed.

NEW: Christopher brings something — a holographic recording from the homeworld. He shows Wikus the planet: oceans, organic cities, the sky a different color. "This is what you are part of. This is what the fluid connected you to." Wikus watches a world he'll never see, that was destroyed before he could reach it. The beauty and loss hit simultaneously.

Christopher tells him about the medical pods. But ALSO: "Vor'thane says you're important. I trust her more than I trust science."

Wikus, looking at the hologram of a dead world: "Important for what? There's nothing left to bridge TO."

Christopher: "There's everything left. That's why we're here."

### Scene 23 — MARCUS GETS THE TIP (pp. 57-58)
Gabriel's information reaches Marcus. Subject Zero is confirmed in the Pond. Marcus activates surveillance. But the hurricane complicates everything — he has 72 hours before the storm makes operations impossible. He pushes up Protocol Harvest's timeline.

### Scene 24 — VOR'THANE AND WIKUS (pp. 58-62)
Aboard Command One. The Tesh'mora revelation. *[Plays as V1 but enhanced:]* 

NEW BODY HORROR: Vor'thane shows Wikus the physical reality of the Bridge Water. An ancient recording: a Poleepkwa undergoing deliberate transformation — not into a human, but into something *else*. The body reshaping, the consciousness expanding. It's beautiful AND horrifying. The Bridge was not a gentle process. It was a shattering and reformation.

Vor'thane: "You were shattered. You reformed. Most beings do not survive that. You are still here. Still *you*. Do you understand how remarkable that is?"

Wikus, honest: "I just thought I was unlucky."

Vor'thane, the first time she laughs: "Luck. Yes. The humans have a concept for everything."

(Vor'thane showing humor — addressing the "Magical Elder" critique.)

### Scene 25 — THE MEDICAL POD (pp. 62-64)
*[Enhanced from V1]* The pod scans Wikus. 15% survival rate. But now the PHYSICAL REALITY is shown: a simulation of what reversal would look like. The exoskeleton dissolving. The internal organs reshaping. The nervous system rewiring. It would take 72 hours and would look like — there's no other word — a man being unmade and remade. The simulation is so viscerally horrible that even the prawn medics look away.

Wikus watches the entire thing. "Fifteen percent," he says. He doesn't say no. He doesn't say yes.

### Scene 26 — TANIA VAN DE MERWE (pp. 64-66)
DOCUMENTARY FORMAT — a new addition. Elena, pursuing the Subject Zero story, tracks down Tania van de Merwe in Johannesburg. Interview via video call.

Tania is 50. She looks tired and strong. She never remarried. She works as a social worker with the remaining Johannesburg prawn community. She knows Wikus is alive — or believes he is. "He leaves me flowers. I've never seen him do it. But every year, there's a flower." She holds up one — a small metal rose, slightly rusty. "I don't know where he is. I don't know what he looks like now. But I know this is his work. Wikus was always... he tried so hard. Even when he was terrible at everything, he tried."

She looks directly at the camera. "If you find him — tell him I understand. Tell him the flowers are enough. Tell him to stop punishing himself."

Elena is destroyed. She ends the call. She stares at the wall. She knows where Wikus is. She knows she can't tell Tania. Not yet.

### Scene 27 — GOVERNOR HARTLEY'S GAMBIT (pp. 66-68)
Hartley on cable news — performing compassion, positioning herself. But NEW: a private moment. Her aide mentions that a prawn child from the raid is in critical condition. Hartley pauses — something human crosses her face. Then: "Make sure we send flowers. And make sure there's a photographer." The aide hesitates. Hartley: "What? Compassion without documentation is just sentiment." She believes this. It's her tragedy.

### Scene 28 — ELENA CONFRONTS WIKUS (pp. 68-72)
*[Enhanced from V1]* Elena tells Wikus she knows. But now with her complicity on the table: "I know who you are. I know what was done to you. And I took money from the people who did it." Her honesty disarms him more than her silence would. Wikus tells his story. The talking-head moment.

NEW LINE: "The funniest part? I used to work for MNU. I used to evict prawns from their homes. Knock on the door, polite as anything: 'Sorry, sir, you need to sign here, we're relocating you.' I did it with a smile. And then God or whoever — God's a prawn, did you know that? Has to be. Only a prawn would think this was funny — turns me INTO one. Cosmic fookin' comedy, that."

Elena, crying, says: "Tania says the flowers are enough. She says to stop punishing yourself."

Wikus goes very still. A very long silence. Then: "She said that?"

"She said that."

Wikus clicks — an involuntary prawn sound, somewhere between a sob and a laugh.

### Scene 29 — HURRICANE CARMEN: CATEGORY 4 (pp. 72-73)
Carmen is Cat 4. Landfall in 36 hours. Evacuation in progress for human neighborhoods. The Pond: "shelter in place." Zara's broadcast: "Shelter in WHAT place? The place you built from cardboard? Or the place we built from ourselves?"

### Scene 30 — WIKUS MAKES HIS CHOICE (pp. 73-76)
*[Enhanced from V1]* Wikus gathers his allies. Reveals himself. The plan to broadcast evidence via Zara's platform during the hurricane.

NEW: Gabriel is present — he's in the Pond, having been sent by Marcus to monitor. He hears the plan. He hesitates. He's been feeding information to Halcyon for three years. But the sterilization evidence... he didn't know about that. He leaves the meeting without committing either way.

### Scene 31 — MARCUS PREPARES (pp. 76-78)
Marcus receives Gabriel's final report. He now knows: Wikus's location, the broadcast plan, and the timing. He also knows Carmen is going to hit. He makes his calculation: execute Protocol Harvest as the hurricane arrives. The storm provides cover, chaos, plausible deniability.

Margaret Yates: "And if there are casualties?" Marcus: "The hurricane killed them. That's what the report will say."

His daughters call from the evacuation hotel. "Daddy, when are you coming?" Marcus: "Soon, sweetheart. Daddy has to work."

### Scene 32 — DARK NIGHT OF THE SOUL (pp. 78-82)
*[Enhanced from V1]* The pre-hurricane night. Same montage structure, but:

- **Wikus** doesn't write Tania's name. Instead, he looks at his prawn face reflected in standing water. He touches the reflection. For the first time, he doesn't flinch. "Okay," he says to himself. Just: "Okay."
- **Gabriel** sits in his apartment. Halcyon vest on the couch. Cat food in his lap. He makes a phone call — to a prawn in the Pond. We don't hear the conversation. He starts crying.
- **Vor'thane**, aboard Command One, performs an ancient ritual — clicking in patterns that cause the ship's organic walls to *pulse*. She's communing with the ship. Or praying. Or both.

### Scene 33 — ZARA'S LAST BROADCAST BEFORE THE STORM (pp. 82-83)
*[Enhanced from V1]* Zara on the roof. But now Kato is with her. They don't speak. They watch the clouds build. Kato, awkwardly, in terrible English: "Is... good view." Zara: "It's a hurricane." Kato: "Yes. Good hurricane view." Zara laughs. It might be the most human moment between two prawns in the film.

### Scene 34 — THE HURRICANE BEGINS / PROTOCOL HARVEST (pp. 83-85)
*[Largely unchanged from V1]* Carmen hits. The assault begins. Military vehicles through the rain.

NEW: Gabriel is in the Pond when the assault begins. He realizes what he's done. He starts running — toward the prawn areas, not away. He's trying to warn people. A Halcyon contractor sees a prawn in a Halcyon vest running the wrong direction and shoots. Gabriel falls. He crawls. He reaches a prawn dwelling and bangs on the door: "They're coming. Get to the swamp. GET TO THE SWAMP." The door opens. The prawn inside sees the Halcyon vest. Spits on him. Then sees the blood. Pulls him inside.

Gabriel dies in a prawn's home. Not his apartment. He dies being cared for by someone who hated him. The prawn doesn't know his name.

END OF ACT II.

## REVISED ACT III: LANDFALL (Pages 86-120)
**17 Scenes — Less institutional resolution, more personal, more ambiguous**

### Scene 35 — CHAOS IN THE POND (pp. 86-89)
*[Enhanced from V1]* Hurricane + assault. But now: the assault is more explicitly targeting Wikus. Halcyon teams have his description (Gabriel's intel). They're going door to door in hurricane conditions, scanning prawns. It's a manhunt inside a natural disaster.

### Scene 36 — KATO'S CALL / FLEET SHUTTLES DESCEND (pp. 89-91)
*[Largely unchanged]* Kato contacts Command One. Armed shuttles descend through the hurricane. But now: Kato has a specific debt — his earlier violence against the contractor. He knows that if he uses weapons now, he becomes the justification for everything. He arms the soldiers anyway. "I'll answer for what I've done. After."

### Scene 37 — WIKUS IN THE STORM (pp. 91-93)
Wikus moves through the hurricane, organizing evacuation to the organic structures. He's in his element — the swamp, the rain, the chaos. He knows every path. He moves like a prawn who's been here for 17 years, because he has. Elena is with him, camera-less (she left it). They haul people through floodwater.

DARK HUMOR: A prawn family's organic dwelling has a human couch inside — stolen/salvaged from a foreclosed house. The couch is floating. The prawn children are using it as a boat. Wikus: "That's actually brilliant." Elena: "It's a couch." Wikus: "It's a BOAT now. Adapt and survive, baby."

### Scene 38 — THE THEME PARK PERIMETER (pp. 93-96)
**NEW SETPIECE.** The hurricane drives some of the conflict toward the western boundary of the Pond — which borders Disney World property. Halcyon contractors pursuing fleeing prawns breach the Disney perimeter. Prawns scatter into the empty park (evacuated for the hurricane).

A pursuit through an abandoned theme park in a Category 4 hurricane. Prawn families hiding in shuttered gift shops. A Halcyon drone navigating through a collapsed roller coaster. A prawn child standing in front of Cinderella Castle in driving rain, looking up at the fantasy spire, while the real world tears itself apart around her.

This is the image of the film. The metaphor made literal. The Happiest Place on Earth as a war zone. Fantasy and reality colliding in a hurricane.

Wikus finds the child. He picks her up and runs. They shelter in a maintenance tunnel under the park. Disney infrastructure — the hidden pipes and wires that make the magic work — surrounding them. The machine behind the curtain.

### Scene 39 — MARCUS AT THE SHUTTLE PAD (pp. 96-98)
*[Largely unchanged]* Marcus assaults the shuttle pad. Christopher is wounded. The hostage play for the Synthesis Engine.

### Scene 40 — ZARA'S BROADCAST (pp. 98-101)
*[Enhanced from V1]* The global broadcast. All evidence deployed. But NEW: Elena's voice is part of it. Elena called Zara and delivered her footage and her confession: "I was funded by Halcyon. I didn't know about the sterilization. But I should have looked harder. This is what they didn't want you to see."

Zara broadcasts it all — the footage, the confession, the sterilization data, the theme park images, Gabriel's death (captured on a Halcyon body cam that a prawn scavenged). The world watches a hurricane, an assault, and a genocide in real time.

### Scene 41 — HARTLEY'S CHOICE (pp. 101-103)
*[Largely unchanged]* Hartley orders the stand-down. But NOW: the National Guard obeys. Halcyon does not. And Hartley's choice is more complex — she DIDN'T know about the sterilization, but she DID know about the raid. She authorized "hurricane security" knowing Marcus would push boundaries. She's not innocent. Her stand-down is damage control AND conscience. She doesn't get to be a hero.

### Scene 42 — MARCUS'S LAST PLAY (pp. 103-105)
*[Enhanced]* Marcus continues. But now his motivation is survival, not greed. If he doesn't secure the Engine, the sterilization evidence destroys Halcyon AND him personally. He's not pursuing profit anymore — he's running from prison. This makes him more dangerous, not less.

### Scene 43 — WIKUS AT THE SHUTTLE PAD (pp. 105-110)
*[Enhanced from V1]* The standoff. Wikus walks between armies. His speech. But now:

He doesn't just speak to Marcus and Kato. He speaks to the camera — Zara's broadcast, live to the world. He knows millions are watching. And he speaks to them:

"My name is Wikus van de Merwe. I am — was — a white South African man. I worked a desk job. I had a wife named Tania who is better than I ever deserved. And one day I became something else. Not by choice. By accident. By the universe's sick sense of humor.

"For seventeen years I tried to go back. To be what I was. And tonight, in a hurricane, on the worst night of my life — again, somehow it's ALWAYS the worst night of my life — I want to tell you something I should have understood years ago.

"I am not what I was. I'm what I am. And what I am is standing here, between two groups of terrified beings who need to stop fookin' shooting at each other.

"So. Please. Just... stop."

Not eloquent. Not polished. Just a tired man — prawn — man — whatever — asking people to stop.

Kato lowers weapons. Marcus... doesn't. He raises his sidearm toward Wikus. But one of his own contractors — a young woman, face visible on the global broadcast, knowing the world is watching — puts her hand on his arm. "Sir. It's over." Marcus looks at her. At the cameras. At Wikus. He lowers the weapon. Not because he's changed. Because he's calculated. Even in his worst moment, he's calculating.

### Scene 44 — THE EYE OF THE STORM (pp. 110-112)
*[Unchanged from V1]* The calm. Stars. Fleet lights. Everyone looking up.

NEW: Okafor treats Christopher. While working, Okafor says: "You know what's funny? In another timeline, I'm just a microbiologist publishing papers nobody reads." Christopher, weak: "In another timeline, I'm a municipal engineer on a planet that still exists." They look at each other. Okafor: "This timeline kind of sucks." Christopher: "The company is better."

### Scene 45 — AFTERMATH: MESSY AND REAL (pp. 112-116)
**DRASTICALLY REVISED.** The resolution is NOT clean:

DOCUMENTARY FORMAT returns, but fragmented — multiple sources, conflicting narratives.

- **Halcyon** is investigated but NOT destroyed. They settle with the federal government. Marcus is arrested, released on bail, and immediately hired by a defense consulting firm. The sterilization program is officially "under review." No one goes to prison in the timeframe of the film.
- **Governor Hartley** calls for a "bipartisan commission on non-human integration." It sounds good. It means nothing yet. She polls 12 points higher nationally.
- **The Non-Human Persons Act** is PROPOSED in Congress. It is immediately filibustered. Zara testifies but the bill dies in committee. On camera, a senator says: "We need more study." The study will take years.
- **Christopher** announces the Synthesis Engine will operate under prawn control only — not joint management. He's done trusting. This pisses off both the US government and some prawn advocates who wanted partnership. Christopher is tired and doesn't care.
- **Command One** remains in orbit. The fleet stays. No one has a plan for what happens next.

The systemic story is UNRESOLVED. The world is marginally better and fundamentally unchanged.

### Scene 46 — OKAFOR AND THE CLINIC (pp. 116-117)
*[Slightly revised]* Okafor's clinic is rebuilt — with fleet medical tech. But the fleet medical pod is for prawns only; it can't treat humans. Okafor works alongside prawn medics. He's accepting help. But he also discovers: the hurricane destroyed his backup copies of the sterilization evidence. Halcyon's lawyers are already arguing the evidence was "compromised" in the storm. The fight isn't over.
