# DISTRICT 10: WORLD BIBLE

## Setting: Orlando, Florida — Present Day (2026)
### ~17 Years After the Events of District 9

---

## 1. THE RETURN

### What Happened on the Homeworld

Christopher Johnson arrived at the Poleepkwa homeworld — designated by human astronomers as Kepler-442b — after a three-year journey at near-light speed. What he found was not the thriving civilization he remembered from fragmented childhood memories. The homeworld was in the final stages of a resource war. Two dominant factions — the *Tesh'ka* (Builders) and the *Vor'kathi* (Harvesters) — had been locked in a generations-long conflict over dwindling reserves of the bio-reactive fluid (what humans call "the fuel"). The ship that had stalled over Johannesburg in 1982 was, in fact, a refugee vessel fleeing this war — a fact Christopher only learned upon his return.

Christopher's arrival with functioning Earth-hybrid fuel technology made him simultaneously invaluable and a target. He spent six years navigating factional politics, brokering a fragile ceasefire, and retrofitting a fleet of 23 vessels with his adapted fuel synthesis. But the ceasefire collapsed. The Vor'kathi faction deployed a bio-weapon that rendered vast agricultural zones sterile. Facing extinction-level famine, Christopher rallied approximately 40,000 Poleepkwa — mostly civilians, some soldiers, many children — into the fleet and fled.

He did not return to Earth as a liberator. He returned as a refugee. Again.

### The Fleet Arrival — March 14, 2026

Twenty-three ships of varying size appeared in low Earth orbit at 3:47 AM Eastern Time on March 14, 2026. The largest — designated "Command One" by NORAD — was approximately 800 meters in diameter, disc-shaped, resembling but distinct from the original Johannesburg mothership. The others ranged from 100 to 400 meters. Unlike the 1982 arrival, these ships were not drifting or disabled. They maintained precise orbital formation.

Within hours, Christopher Johnson broadcast a message on all radio frequencies, in both Poleepkwa clicks and heavily accented English:

*"This is Christopher Johnson. I left Earth seventeen years ago with a promise. I have returned. We are not soldiers. We are families. We need shelter. Please."*

The message was recorded by amateur radio operators, leaked to social media within minutes, and became the most-viewed video in internet history within 24 hours. Governments scrambled. Markets crashed. The UN Security Council convened an emergency session.

### Why Orlando

The fleet did not land in Johannesburg. Christopher had learned from the original disaster. South Africa's government, now openly hostile to Poleepkwa, had constructed orbital defense platforms purchased from Chinese contractors. Europe and East Asia similarly rejected entry.

The United States, under a pragmatic administration seeking to control the narrative (and the technology), negotiated a controlled landing zone: a 50-square-mile tract of undeveloped swampland and decommissioned military facilities in **Orange and Osceola Counties, Florida** — specifically, land south of Orlando International Airport, east of Walt Disney World property, and north of the former Deseret Ranch.

The official reasoning:
- **Cape Canaveral proximity**: NASA and Space Force infrastructure for monitoring and potential technology exchange
- **Military infrastructure**: The former McCoy Air Force Base (now Orlando International) and Naval Training Center Orlando (decommissioned but structurally intact) provided ready-made containment facilities
- **Climate**: Poleepkwa biology thrives in hot, humid environments. Florida's subtropical climate was the closest match to homeworld conditions in the continental US
- **Political calculation**: Florida's governor, eyeing a presidential run, saw an opportunity to position himself as the strongman managing the "alien crisis"

The unofficial reasoning everyone understood: Orlando's tourism industry was still recovering from post-pandemic economic damage. The theme parks needed cheap labor. The construction industry needed bodies. Florida's existing infrastructure for managing large transient populations (tourists, seasonal workers, undocumented immigrants) could be repurposed.

## 2. THE WORLD SINCE DISTRICT 9

### Global Prawn Politics

The 17 years since Johannesburg transformed global politics. Key developments:

**The Johannesburg Aftermath (2010-2015):**
After the mothership departed, the remaining 1.8 million Poleepkwa in District 10 (the relocation camp) became South Africa's most contentious political issue. International pressure led to marginal improvements in conditions, but the camps remained functionally open-air prisons. A 2013 uprising — the "District 10 Riots" — resulted in 400 prawn deaths and 23 human casualties, leading to international sanctions against South Africa.

**The Dispersion (2015-2020):**
Under various UN agreements, Poleepkwa populations were "redistributed" globally. In practice, this meant nations accepting small quotas of prawns in exchange for access to reverse-engineered alien technology. Key populations:
- **United States**: ~200,000 prawns, mostly in Gulf Coast states (Texas, Louisiana, Florida)
- **Brazil**: ~150,000, in Amazon-adjacent settlements
- **India**: ~300,000, in purpose-built camps in Rajasthan
- **Australia**: ~100,000, in the Northern Territory
- **China**: ~250,000 (officially; actual numbers suspected higher), in classified facilities in Xinjiang

**Legal Status:**
The 2018 UN Resolution on Non-Human Persons ("The Prawn Accords") established Poleepkwa as "sentient entities with conditional rights" — a deliberately vague framework. In practice, rights vary wildly by nation. In the US, prawns exist in a legal gray zone similar to undocumented immigrants: technically protected from murder, practically vulnerable to everything else.

### MNU's Evolution

Multi-National United (MNU) collapsed as a corporate entity in 2014, destroyed by leaked documents detailing their weapons experiments on live Poleepkwa subjects (the "Wikus Files," leaked by an anonymous source). However, MNU's privatized alien-management infrastructure was too valuable to disappear.

**Halcyon Solutions Group** (HSG) emerged from MNU's ashes in 2016. Publicly, Halcyon brands itself as a "humanitarian logistics" company — managing prawn housing, employment programs, and "integration services." Privately, Halcyon is MNU with better PR. They hold exclusive US government contracts for:
- Prawn population management and tracking (mandatory bio-chips implanted in all registered prawns)
- Alien technology research and development (black-budget programs)
- Security services for prawn-dense areas
- "Voluntary" labor placement programs (functionally indentured servitude)

Halcyon's CEO, **Margaret Yates**, is a former MNU middle manager who survived the company's collapse by cooperating with investigators — and destroying evidence that implicated her personally.

### Technology

Seventeen years of reverse-engineering prawn technology has yielded:
- **Medical advances**: Prawn bio-gel accelerates wound healing in humans by 400%. A billion-dollar pharmaceutical industry has emerged. Prawns are farmed for their biological secretions
- **Energy**: Limited success replicating prawn fuel technology. The fluid requires biological components that can only be synthesized by living Poleepkwa, creating a grim dependency
- **Weapons**: The US, China, and Russia all have classified programs. The iconic prawn lightning gun has been partially replicated, though human versions are unreliable and prone to catastrophic malfunction
- **Computing**: Prawn neural-processing architecture has advanced quantum computing by a decade

## 3. ORLANDO — THE NEW DISTRICT

### Geography of Exclusion

The **Orlando Poleepkwa Processing Zone (OPPZ)**, colloquially called **"The Pond"** (a mocking reference to prawns and Florida's swamps), occupies a roughly triangular area:
- **North boundary**: The 528 (Bee Line Expressway)
- **West boundary**: Florida's Turnpike
- **South/East boundary**: Unmarked, fading into swampland and cattle ranches

Within the Pond, approximately 60,000 pre-existing Florida prawns and the initial wave of 40,000 fleet arrivals are housed in a mixture of:
- Converted warehouse complexes along Boggy Creek Road
- Hastily erected prefab housing blocks (Halcyon-contracted, already deteriorating)
- Self-constructed prawn shelters in the wetland margins — elaborate structures built from scavenged materials and prawn bio-secretions, resembling organic coral formations growing out of the swamp

The contrast is surreal and deliberate: the OPPZ sits **eight miles from Walt Disney World's Magic Kingdom**. On clear nights, prawns in the Pond can see the fireworks over Cinderella Castle.

### The Tourism-Industrial Complex

Orlando's theme parks — Disney, Universal, SeaWorld — initially resisted prawn labor. Bad optics. But economics won. By 2024, an estimated 15,000 prawns worked in Orlando's tourism infrastructure:
- **Construction**: Prawns are immensely strong and can work in Florida's brutal heat without breaks. They built Universal's newest park expansion in half the projected time
- **Maintenance**: Underwater work (prawns are amphibious), hazardous waste cleanup, mechanical repair in confined spaces
- **Behind the scenes**: Kitchen work, laundry, groundskeeping — never customer-facing

This is all managed through Halcyon's labor placement program, which pays prawns in "community credits" redeemable only at Halcyon-operated stores within the Pond. The parallel to company scrip and company towns is exact and intentional.

A controversial **"Encounter Zone"** tourist attraction operates on International Drive — a sanitized, air-conditioned facility where tourists can "meet real aliens" in controlled settings. Prawns working the Encounter Zone are paid better than average and coached to perform "alien behaviors" for visitors. It is, depending on your perspective, cultural exchange or a human zoo.

### Florida Specifics

**Climate**: Poleepkwa thrive in heat and humidity. While humans wilt in Florida's August, prawns become more active, more social, more vocal. Their exoskeletons actually absorb moisture from the air, giving them an iridescent sheen in high humidity. The swamps suit them biologically in a way Johannesburg's dry climate never did. Some xenobiologists theorize the homeworld was swamp-dominant.

**Hurricanes**: Prawn bio-architecture is remarkably hurricane-resistant. Their organic structures flex rather than break, re-seal themselves after damage, and actually incorporate water infiltration into their design. After Hurricane Elias (Category 4, 2025), the Pond's prawn-built structures survived largely intact while surrounding human construction was devastated. This sparked both admiration and resentment — and conspiracy theories about prawns "controlling weather."

**The Swamps**: Prawns have colonized the wetlands in ways humans never could. They've established semi-aquatic communities in the deeper swamp areas beyond the official Pond boundaries. These "wild" prawns — unregistered, unchipped — are a constant source of tension. Halcyon conducts regular "sweeps" to round them up. The prawns call these communities *Tesh'va* — "free water."

**Immigration Parallels**: Florida's existing tensions around immigration — Cuban, Haitian, Central American — map directly onto the prawn crisis. The same politicians who railed against human immigration now rail louder against prawn presence. The same advocacy networks that protected undocumented humans now extend to prawns. The rhetoric is identical; only the species has changed.

## 4. ALIEN TECHNOLOGY & BIOLOGY

### The Fleet's Technology

Christopher's fleet brought technology more advanced than what the original mothership carried:
- **Medical pods**: Can heal most prawn injuries and diseases. A few can interface with human biology (this becomes plot-critical)
- **Communication arrays**: Quantum-entangled systems allowing instant communication between ships and ground. Humans cannot intercept or decrypt these signals, which terrifies intelligence agencies
- **Defensive weapons**: The fleet ships carry energy weapons capable of devastating a city block. Christopher has kept these powered down as a gesture of peace — but everyone knows they're there
- **The Synthesis Engine**: A device on Command One that can produce the bio-reactive fluid in large quantities. This is the single most valuable object on Earth. Every government and corporation wants it. Christopher guards it with his life

### Prawn Biology Updates

- Poleepkwa lifespan appears to be 80-120 Earth years. Christopher, at roughly 50, is middle-aged
- Wikus, transformed 17 years ago, has fully integrated into prawn biology but retains anomalies: his neural patterns show human-like activity, he processes language differently than born-prawns, and his exoskeleton has a slightly different coloration (mottled grey-green rather than standard brown)
- The transformation fluid is now understood to be a **biological integration agent** — it was originally designed to allow Poleepkwa to merge with their technology (bio-mechanical interfaces). Its effect on humans was an unintended and poorly understood side effect
- Prawns born on Earth ("Terrapkwa" in prawn language, "Mudprawns" in human slang) show subtle differences from homeworld-born prawns: slightly smaller, adapted to Earth gravity, and culturally distinct — they've never known anything but camps and human hostility

### The Fluid

The bio-reactive fluid remains central. Christopher's Synthesis Engine can produce it, but it requires biological input — specifically, cellular material from living Poleepkwa. This creates a horrifying economic pressure: the fluid is the basis of both prawn technology AND the human pharmaceutical industry that profits from prawn bio-gel. Halcyon is intensely interested in the Synthesis Engine because it would allow them to industrialize fluid production without depending on Christopher's cooperation.

## 5. SOCIAL DYNAMICS

### Human-Prawn Relations in Orlando

A spectrum exists:
- **Hostile segregationists**: "Send them back" / "The Pond is too close to my kids' school" / organized militia groups patrolling Pond boundaries
- **Pragmatic exploiters**: Business owners, politicians, and Halcyon itself — they want prawns present but controlled, productive but powerless
- **Activists and allies**: A small but vocal prawn-rights movement, drawing from immigration advocacy, civil rights tradition, and religious communities. Often called "shrimp-huggers" derisively
- **Indifferent majority**: Most Orlandoans have adjusted to prawns as a fact of life. They don't think about the Pond unless traffic is bad or there's an "incident" on the news

### Prawn Culture on Earth

Seventeen years have produced a unique Poleepkwa-Earth hybrid culture:
- **Language**: Most Earth-born prawns are bilingual — Poleepkwa clicks and English/Spanish (Florida's linguistic mix has influenced them). Some have developed a pidgin that human linguists call "Clickish"
- **Food**: Cat food remains a staple (it contains taurine, which is biologically necessary for Poleepkwa). But prawns have also developed tastes for human food — particularly raw seafood, citrus fruits, and, inexplicably, ranch dressing
- **Music**: Prawns have a rich sonic culture. Their clicks and vocalizations have been incorporated into a underground music scene — "Clickstep" — that has human fans. Prawn musicians collaborate with human electronic producers
- **Religion**: Some prawns have adopted elements of human religion; some humans have become interested in Poleepkwa spiritual practices (ancestor memory, communal consciousness rituals). A syncretic faith called "The Tide" has emerged in the Pond
- **Sports**: Prawns are banned from human sports leagues (obviously), but informal prawn athletic competitions in the Pond draw thousands of human spectators who watch via drone-streamed video

### The Cat Food Economy

Taurine-rich cat food remains the most reliable Poleepkwa nutrition source. Major pet food companies now quietly produce "industrial grade" product specifically for prawn consumption — it's their most profitable line. Cat food is used as informal currency in the Pond, alongside Halcyon community credits. Black market cat food operations are the prawn equivalent of drug trafficking — not because cat food is illegal, but because Halcyon controls official distribution and uses it as leverage.

## 6. THE DOCUMENTARY/MEDIA LANDSCAPE

### The Original Documentary

The MNU-commissioned documentary about District 9 was suppressed after MNU's collapse but leaked in its entirety in 2015. It became one of the most-watched documentaries in history — "the Zapruder film of first contact." Wikus van de Merwe's face (human face) became iconic: the symbol of what MNU did, the face of transformation, the last known human-to-prawn convert.

### Present-Day Media

**Mainstream**: 24-hour cable news covers the fleet arrival and the Pond with the same breathless, reductive coverage they apply to everything. Fox, CNN, and MSNBC each have their prawn "angle." Conservative media frames it as invasion. Liberal media frames it as refugee crisis. Neither captures the reality.

**Social Media**: Prawns are online. Earth-born prawns especially — they grew up with smartphones (cheap, cracked, shared among dozens). Prawn TikTok accounts have millions of followers. The most famous, @PrawnLife, run by a young prawn who posts daily life in the Pond, has 22 million followers and a merch line. Instagram influencers post selfies at the Encounter Zone. The discourse is constant and toxic.

**Documentary/Journalism**: Several documentary crews are in Orlando. The most prominent is a team led by a young South African filmmaker whose parents were Johannesburg journalists who covered the original District 9. This filmmaker is making a direct sequel documentary, trying to find Wikus and tell the "real story." This becomes our framing device.

**Halcyon's Media Machine**: Halcyon employs a sophisticated PR operation. They produce slick "integration success stories," fund sympathetic media coverage, and aggressively suppress negative reporting. They have an army of social media bots and paid influencers. They are also, through subsidiary companies, the majority funder of the Encounter Zone.

### Our Film's Framing

DISTRICT 10 uses a similar approach to the original film: it begins as a documentary, with talking-head interviews, news footage, and handheld camera work. As the story intensifies, the documentary frame fractures — the filmmakers become part of the story, the camera becomes a participant rather than observer, and the film gradually transitions into a more cinematic, immersive style. By Act III, the documentary conceit has almost entirely dissolved, just as it did in District 9.

Key documentary elements:
- Interviews with politicians, Halcyon executives, prawn-rights activists, and ordinary humans
- Found footage from prawn smartphones and surveillance cameras
- News broadcasts covering escalating events
- The filmmaker's personal journey and increasing involvement
- Archival footage from the original District 9 documentary

## 7. FLORIDA-SPECIFIC ELEMENTS

### Hurricane Season
The story takes place in late August / early September — peak hurricane season. A developing tropical system in the Gulf becomes a ticking clock: if a major hurricane hits Orlando, the Pond's human-built infrastructure will be devastated while prawn structures survive, creating a humanitarian crisis AND a visual metaphor for which approach to building (and living) actually works.

### The Swamp
The Poleepkwa's relationship with Florida's wetlands is transformative — literally. Their biological secretions interact with the swamp ecosystem, accelerating plant growth, creating bioluminescent algae blooms, and attracting wildlife. The swamp around prawn settlements becomes almost alien — a hybrid ecosystem that's neither fully terrestrial nor fully Poleepkwa. It's beautiful and unsettling. Alligators coexist with prawns (both apex semi-aquatic predators; they've reached an uneasy mutual respect).

### Florida Man Headlines
The story acknowledges Florida's reputation. News chyrons throughout the film include:
- "FLORIDA MAN ATTEMPTS TO SELL PRAWN EGGS ON FACEBOOK MARKETPLACE"
- "ALLIGATOR FOUND IN PRAWN HOUSING UNIT, APPARENTLY DOMESTICATED"
- "ORLANDO THEME PARK PRAWN WORKER FIRED FOR BEING 'TOO FRIENDLY' TO GUESTS"
- "STAND YOUR GROUND CITED IN PRAWN SHOOTING, LEGAL EXPERTS DIVIDED"

### Cape Canaveral Connection
The fleet's orbit is monitored from Cape Canaveral, 50 miles east. The Space Force maintains a constant presence, and the tension between military monitoring and Christopher's fleet creates a secondary conflict. There's a direct narrative line from Cape Canaveral's history of launching humans into space to its new role monitoring aliens who arrived from space — the American space dream, inverted.

### Stand Your Ground
Florida's self-defense laws become a plot point when a human kills a prawn and claims Stand Your Ground. The legal question — does Stand Your Ground apply to non-humans? — becomes a national flashpoint and accelerates the story's political tensions.

### The Theme Park Metaphor
Walt Disney World's presence eight miles from the Pond is not subtle symbolism — it's blunt, deliberate irony. The Happiest Place on Earth next to a refugee camp. Animatronic aliens entertaining children while real aliens live in squalor nearby. A theme park built on the fantasy of other worlds, adjacent to actual beings from another world. The film will use this juxtaposition visually and thematically throughout.
