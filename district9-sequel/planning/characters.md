# DISTRICT 10: CHARACTER PROFILES

---

## RETURNING CHARACTERS

---

### WIKUS VAN DE MERWE
**Species:** Poleepkwa (formerly human) | **Age:** 52 (human equivalent) | **Status:** Alive, hiding

#### Background
Wikus completed his transformation into a Poleepkwa within weeks of Christopher's departure in 2009. For the first three years, he was held in a secret MNU laboratory — designated "Subject Zero" — where researchers attempted to reverse-engineer the transformation process. When MNU collapsed in 2014, the facility was hastily decommissioned. Wikus was slated for termination, but a sympathetic lab technician (who later died in unrelated circumstances) left his containment unlocked.

Wikus escaped into the South African prawn population of District 10. For three years, he lived as an anonymous prawn — learning to use his new body, sinking into depression, surviving on cat food and the kindness of strangers. When the global redistribution began in 2015, he was processed (under a false identity — prawns all look alike to human administrators) and shipped to the United States. He ended up in Florida.

For the last eleven years, Wikus has lived in the Orlando prawn community — first in early settlements, then in the Pond when it was established. He is one of the few prawns who speaks fluent, nuanced English and Afrikaans. He has become an informal mediator between prawns and human administrators, though he keeps a low profile to avoid identification. Halcyon has a standing bounty for "Subject Zero" — the only known human-to-prawn convert — though they officially deny his existence.

#### Psychological State
Wikus is profoundly broken and profoundly adapted. He has accepted his prawn body — he had no choice — but he has not accepted the loss of his humanity. He still thinks in English. He still dreams of Tania. Every year on their anniversary, he leaves a small metal flower (fashioned from scrap) outside the Pond's fence, pointed toward South Africa.

He is respected in the Pond community but not truly part of it. Born-prawns sense something different about him — his movements are slightly wrong, his clicks carry odd inflections, his emotional responses are not quite Poleepkwa. He is an immigrant among immigrants, an outsider among outsiders.

He has developed a dark, sardonic humor — a coping mechanism. He narrates his life internally as if he's still being filmed for a documentary. He is deeply kind to prawn children but avoids close adult relationships. He drinks taurine-heavy "prawn beer" (fermented cat food — it's as bad as it sounds) too much.

#### WANT (External Goal)
When Christopher's fleet arrives, Wikus wants one thing: to get aboard Command One and use the medical technology to reverse his transformation. He wants to be human again. He wants to go home to Tania.

#### NEED (Internal Growth)
Wikus needs to accept that "home" is not a species or a body — it's where you choose to belong. He needs to stop running from what he is and recognize that his unique position (human mind, prawn body) makes him the one person who can bridge the two worlds. His transformation was not a curse. It was a calling.

#### Arc
Wikus begins the film hiding, surviving, dreaming of reversal. Christopher's return reignites hope. But as he gets drawn into the escalating crisis, he's forced to step out of hiding, reveal himself, and become the voice for a people he never chose to join but has come to love. By the end, he makes the choice to remain a prawn — not because he can't change back, but because his people need him more than he needs his old life.

The metal flower he leaves at the fence — in the final scene, he leaves one inside the Pond. He's home.

### CHRISTOPHER JOHNSON
**Species:** Poleepkwa | **Age:** ~50 (middle-aged by prawn standards) | **Status:** Fleet Commander

#### Background
Christopher's seventeen-year journey transformed him from a desperate father into a reluctant leader. On the homeworld, he discovered the truth about the Johannesburg ship — it was a refugee vessel, not an exploration craft — and about the civil war that had consumed Poleepkwa civilization. He spent years navigating factional politics, losing friends, making compromises that haunt him, and ultimately failing to save the homeworld.

He returned to Earth carrying guilt: he promised to come back and help the Earth prawns. He's kept that promise. But he's also brought 40,000 more mouths to feed to a planet that didn't want the first million.

#### His Son — LITTLE CJ (now "KATO")
Christopher's son, now a young adult prawn (~20 in human terms), has taken the name Kato. He grew up on ships and on a dying homeworld. He has never known peace. He is angry, militant, and distrustful of humans in a way his father is not. Their relationship is strained: Kato sees his father as too willing to negotiate, too soft, too marked by his time among humans. Christopher sees in Kato the very Poleepkwa militarism that destroyed the homeworld.

#### WANT (External Goal)
Christopher wants to secure permanent, safe settlement for his people on Earth. This means negotiating with the US government, managing Halcyon's exploitation, keeping his own fleet's military hawks in check, and somehow finding enough fluid to sustain his people. He also wants to find Wikus — the debt he owes is personal and deep.

#### NEED (Internal Growth)
Christopher needs to accept that he cannot save everyone, and that trying to control everything (the fleet, the negotiations, his son) is a form of the same authoritarianism he fled. He needs to trust others — including humans — and accept that peace requires vulnerability.

#### Arc
Christopher begins as a controlled, diplomatic leader maintaining a fragile peace. Events force escalation. His son's actions threaten to spark a war. Halcyon's betrayal destroys the negotiation path. Christopher must choose between using his fleet's weapons (becoming the invader humans fear) or finding another way. He finds another way — but it costs him.

## NEW HUMAN CHARACTERS

---

### ELENA VASQUEZ
**Role:** Documentarian/Filmmaker | **Age:** 29 | **Nationality:** South African (Cape Town)

#### Background
Elena's parents were journalists in Johannesburg who covered the original District 9 events. Her mother filmed some of the leaked MNU footage that eventually destroyed the company. Her father was killed in the District 10 Riots of 2013 — shot by a private security contractor (later identified as MNU-affiliated). Elena grew up in the shadow of the prawn story. It defined her family, destroyed it, and gave her a purpose.

She is a rising documentary filmmaker who has spent two years obtaining permits, access, and funding to make a documentary about the Orlando Pond — conceived as a follow-up to the original Johannesburg documentary. When the fleet arrives, her small project becomes the biggest story on Earth.

Elena is idealistic but not naive. She has her mother's eye for hidden truths and her father's stubbornness. She is bilingual (English/Afrikaans), has taught herself basic Poleepkwa clicks, and has genuine relationships with prawn community members. She is our primary POV character for the documentary framing.

#### Personality
Driven, empathetic, occasionally self-righteous. She believes in the power of documentation — that showing the truth changes things. She is about to learn that some truths are too big for a camera, and that being an observer becomes impossible when the world is on fire.

#### WANT
Make the definitive documentary about the fleet arrival and human-prawn relations. Find Wikus van de Merwe — the holy grail for any journalist covering this story. Win the recognition her father never got.

#### NEED
Elena needs to learn that some moments require you to put down the camera and act. Her father died with a camera in his hand. She doesn't have to. The story isn't the film — the story is the people.

#### Arc
Elena begins as our guide, our documentary framing device. She interviews subjects, gathers footage, maintains journalistic distance. As the crisis escalates, that distance becomes impossible. She discovers Wikus's identity. She forms a bond with him. When violence erupts, she must choose between filming the definitive shot and helping the people she's come to care about. She chooses the people. Her camera, dropped in the climax, is picked up by someone else — the documentary continues without her, because the story is bigger than any filmmaker.

#### Key Relationships
- **Wikus**: Initially a subject, becomes a friend, almost a father figure. She sees in him what her father might have become
- **Christopher**: A formal interview subject who gradually reveals vulnerability
- **Marcus Bridwell**: Antagonistic — he sees her as a threat, she sees him as the story's villain
- **Zara**: Friendship and mutual respect; Zara gives her access, she gives Zara a platform

### MARCUS BRIDWELL
**Role:** Halcyon Solutions Group, Director of Florida Operations | **Age:** 48 | **Nationality:** American (Virginia)

#### Background
Marcus is a career corporate security/intelligence professional — ex-CIA, then Blackwater (before it rebranded), then private sector defense contracting, then Halcyon. He is not a cartoon villain. He is a system, personified.

Marcus genuinely believes that managing the prawn population through corporate efficiency is better than the alternatives (military occupation, chaotic integration, or — worst case — interspecies war). He has seen what happens when "managing populations" goes wrong (Iraq, Afghanistan). He believes Halcyon's approach — controlled labor, structured housing, bio-chipping, gradual integration on human terms — is the *humane* option.

He is wrong, of course. But he is wrong in the way that intelligent, experienced people are wrong: confidently, systematically, and with PowerPoint presentations to prove it.

#### Personality
Calm, articulate, professional. Never raises his voice. Wears khakis and polo shirts (it's Florida). Has a wife and two daughters in Winter Park. Coaches his daughter's soccer team on weekends. Is also directly responsible for policies that have killed prawns through neglect, medical experimentation, and "population management" measures that amount to forced sterilization.

The banality of evil. Hannah Arendt in a golf shirt.

#### WANT
Secure Halcyon's contract to manage the fleet's integration. This means controlling the Synthesis Engine (or at least controlling access to it). The fleet arrival is the biggest business opportunity in Halcyon's history — potentially trillions of dollars in alien technology, labor, and biological resources. Marcus wants to deliver this for his company and, not incidentally, for his own career.

#### NEED
Marcus needs to confront the human cost of his "management" philosophy. He needs to see the prawns not as a population to be managed but as people. He will not complete this arc — he is the antagonist. But the film will show the cracks: moments where his certainty wavers, where he sees a prawn child and hesitates, where the system he serves asks him to do something that even he recognizes as monstrous.

#### Arc
Marcus begins as the reasonable face of Halcyon — the guy doing interviews with Elena's camera, talking about "partnership" and "integration pathways." As the crisis escalates, his reasonable mask slips. The corporate pressure to secure the Synthesis Engine pushes him toward increasingly brutal measures. By Act III, he has authorized military action against the Pond, and the man who coaches soccer is directing an assault that will kill hundreds. He does not become a different person. The system simply reveals what he always was.

#### Key Relationships
- **Margaret Yates**: His boss, offscreen pressure (video calls, directives)
- **Elena**: He initially cooperates with her documentary (good PR). Later, he tries to suppress it
- **Christopher**: Negotiation adversary. Marcus underestimates Christopher's intelligence
- **Governor Hartley**: Political ally and mutual user

### GOVERNOR PATRICIA "PAT" HARTLEY
**Role:** Governor of Florida | **Age:** 55 | **Nationality:** American (Jacksonville)

#### Background
A former state attorney general who won the governorship on a "law and order" platform that was implicitly (and sometimes explicitly) anti-prawn. She is smart, charismatic, and ruthlessly ambitious — eyeing the presidency. The fleet arrival is either the crisis that destroys her career or the crisis she rides to the White House.

#### Personality
Media-savvy, calculated, capable of genuine warmth (when cameras are on). She doesn't hate prawns — she doesn't think about them enough to hate them. They are a political problem to be leveraged. She will protect them when it serves her and sacrifice them when it doesn't.

#### WANT
Manage the fleet crisis in a way that launches her presidential bid. This means appearing strong but compassionate, decisive but diplomatic. She wants to be the governor who "solved the alien crisis."

#### NEED
To see the prawns as constituents, not props. She won't fully get there, but a crisis moment forces her to make one genuine moral choice.

#### Arc
Hartley maneuvers politically throughout Acts I and II, appearing on cable news, staging photo ops at the Pond, playing Halcyon and the federal government against each other. In Act III, when everything goes wrong, she has a choice: double down on the militarized response (politically safe) or intervene to stop the violence (politically risky). She makes the risky choice — not out of pure altruism, but because Elena's footage has gone viral and the political calculus has shifted. Her best moment is also her most calculating. That ambiguity is the point.

### DR. JAMES OKAFOR
**Role:** Xenobiologist / Prawn Rights Activist | **Age:** 42 | **Nationality:** Nigerian-American (Houston, TX)

#### Background
James was a promising microbiologist at Johns Hopkins when the prawn redistribution began. Fascinated by Poleepkwa biology, he transitioned to xenobiology and quickly became one of the world's foremost experts on prawn physiology. But unlike most of his colleagues, James spent time IN the prawn communities — not just studying samples in labs. He learned to speak basic Poleepkwa. He saw the conditions. He became radicalized.

James now runs a medical clinic inside the Pond — technically a Halcyon-sanctioned facility, but James routinely defies Halcyon protocols to provide care they've deemed "non-essential." He is the prawns' doctor, advocate, and, increasingly, political ally. He is also secretly documenting Halcyon's forced sterilization program and feeding information to Elena.

#### Personality
Warm, patient, exhausted. He has the dark humor of a war-zone medic. He curses fluently in Igbo when frustrated. He hasn't had a vacation in four years. He is exactly the kind of person who burns out and breaks down, and the film will push him to that edge.

#### WANT
Expose Halcyon's sterilization program and secure proper medical care for the Pond's population. With the fleet's arrival, he also wants access to the medical pods aboard the ships — technology that could save thousands of prawn lives.

#### NEED
James needs to accept help. He has a savior complex — he carries everything himself because he doesn't trust anyone else to care enough. He needs to let the prawns fight their own battles with him, not for them.

#### Arc
James is our moral compass. He begins overwhelmed but functioning, the one human the prawns genuinely trust. The fleet's arrival brings hope (medical technology) and chaos (more patients, more Halcyon scrutiny). When Halcyon shuts down his clinic as a "security measure," he has to choose between compliance and resistance. He chooses resistance, and it costs him — arrested, discredited, his evidence nearly destroyed. But his documentation, combined with Elena's footage, becomes the evidence that turns public opinion.

## NEW PRAWN CHARACTERS

---

### ZARA (Poleepkwa name: *Zer'aka*)
**Role:** Earth-born Prawn Community Leader | **Age:** 16 (young adult by prawn biology) | **Nickname:** Zara

#### Background
Zara was born in District 10, Johannesburg, in 2010 — one year after Christopher's departure. She has never seen the homeworld. She has never seen the sky without fences. She was "redistributed" to the US at age five and has lived in Florida prawn settlements since.

Zara is the voice of the Terrapkwa — the Earth-born generation. She speaks English and Poleepkwa with equal fluency, code-switching effortlessly. She runs @PrawnLife, the massively popular social media account that documents daily life in the Pond. She is funny, fierce, and deeply political in the way that young people who've grown up in systems of oppression often are — she has no patience for respectability politics or "gradual integration."

#### Personality
Charismatic, impulsive, brave to the point of recklessness. She has the energy of a born activist and the media savvy of someone who grew up online. She is also sixteen, with all the emotional volatility that implies. She is terrified of the fleet prawns — these alien aliens who share her species but not her world — and this fear manifests as hostility.

#### WANT
Legal rights for Earth-born prawns. She wants the vote, property rights, freedom of movement — full legal personhood. The fleet's arrival threatens this: if humans see 40,000 new "invaders," the political window for Earth-prawn rights slams shut.

#### NEED
Zara needs to connect with her heritage. She has rejected everything Poleepkwa — the homeworld, the fleet, the "old ways" — because embracing them means acknowledging that Earth is not her true home. Meeting Christopher and the fleet prawns forces her to reconcile her Earth identity with her Poleepkwa biology.

#### Arc
Zara begins as a vocal opponent of the fleet ("We were here first, we've earned our place, they're going to ruin everything"). She clashes with Kato (Christopher's son), who represents everything she resents — a "real" prawn from the homeworld who looks down on Terrapkwa. Their animosity evolves into grudging respect, then alliance, then something that might be love (or its prawn equivalent — a deep psychic bonding called *tesh'ken*). By the end, Zara is broadcasting the climactic events live to millions, using her platform to do what Elena's camera cannot — reach the people directly.

### KATO (Poleepkwa name: *Kat'ori* — Christopher's son, "Little CJ")
**Role:** Fleet Military Liaison / Hothead | **Age:** ~20 (young adult)

#### Background
Kato barely remembers Earth. He grew up on ships and on a dying homeworld, raised by soldiers after Christopher was consumed by politics. He is a product of war — trained to fight, suspicious of peace, contemptuous of weakness. He views the Earth prawns as degraded — contaminated by human culture, addicted to cat food, speaking mongrel language.

He is also his father's son: underneath the militant exterior is someone desperately seeking belonging. The homeworld is gone. Earth is hostile. The fleet is all he has, and he'll fight to protect it.

#### Personality
Intense, physical, proud. He moves with military precision. He speaks English poorly and refuses to improve (a point of pride). He is fascinated and repelled by Earth culture in equal measure. He is, essentially, an immigrant's kid in reverse — a Poleepkwa raised in Poleepkwa culture who must navigate an alien (to him) world.

#### WANT
Protect the fleet. Secure territory. If humans won't give them a home willingly, take one. He represents the militant option Christopher is trying to avoid.

#### NEED
To understand that strength without connection is just violence. His father's path — negotiation, vulnerability, trust — is not weakness. Meeting Zara (an Earth prawn who is everything he disdains) forces him to question his certainty.

#### Arc
Kato begins as the threat — the reason humans are afraid, the voice saying "we should use the weapons." He clashes with Christopher (publicly undermining negotiations), clashes with Zara (cultural clash and mutual contempt), and becomes increasingly dangerous as Halcyon's provocations give him justification. In Act II's crisis, his actions nearly start a war. In Act III, Zara's livestream shows him the human (and prawn) cost of violence, and he makes the choice his father hoped for — he stands down. Not because he's weak, but because he's finally strong enough to choose peace.

### THE ELDER — *VOR'THANE*
**Role:** Fleet Elder / Spiritual Leader | **Age:** ~110 (ancient by prawn standards)

#### Background
Vor'thane is the oldest surviving Poleepkwa from the original homeworld's golden age. She (prawn gender is fluid, but Vor'thane uses she/her in human interactions) remembers the time before the civil war, before the refugee ships, before Earth. She is a keeper of history — an oral tradition holder whose memory contains centuries of Poleepkwa culture, science, and philosophy.

She came on the fleet because the homeworld's memories must not die. She is frail in body but immense in presence. When she speaks, other prawns fall silent.

#### Personality
Patient, cryptic, occasionally terrifying. She sees patterns across centuries. She is not sentimental. She is the one who tells Christopher hard truths he doesn't want to hear.

#### WANT
To ensure Poleepkwa civilization survives — not just biologically but culturally. She views Earth as potentially the new homeworld, but only if the Poleepkwa can remember who they were.

#### NEED
To accept that survival sometimes means transformation. The Poleepkwa on Earth are different — and that's okay. Culture that can't evolve is already dead.

#### Arc
Vor'thane is a supporting character but a crucial one. She recognizes Wikus for what he is — a being transformed — and tells him something that changes his understanding: the transformation fluid was not an accident. In Poleepkwa history, chosen individuals underwent deliberate transformation to become bridges between different forms of consciousness. Wikus is not a mistake. He is the fulfillment of an ancient practice. This revelation reframes the entire story.

## THE ANTAGONIST(S)

### Primary Antagonist: MARCUS BRIDWELL (see above)
Marcus is the primary human antagonist — the face of systemic oppression.

### Secondary Antagonist: HALCYON SOLUTIONS GROUP (systemic)
The corporation itself is an antagonist — its policies, incentive structures, and institutional logic drive the conflict even when individual actors (like Marcus) have moments of doubt.

### Tertiary Antagonist: FEAR ITSELF
The film's deepest antagonist is fear — human fear of the alien, prawn fear of humanity, Christopher's fear of failure, Wikus's fear of himself. Every character acts, at some point, out of fear, and every terrible decision in the story flows from it.

### Wild Card: KATO
Kato is not a villain but he is an antagonistic force in Acts I and II — his militarism threatens to give humans exactly the justification they need for a violent response.
