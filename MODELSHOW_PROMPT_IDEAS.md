# ModelShow (mdls) Prompt Ideas

## 🎯 Categories of Prompts

### 1. **Creative & Artistic**
- "Write a haiku about artificial intelligence that also works as a Python function"
- "Design a fictional alien creature with unique biological adaptations for a desert planet"
- "Create a short story premise where time flows backwards for one character"
- "Invent a new board game concept that combines strategy with storytelling"
- "Write a poem in the style of Emily Dickinson about modern technology"

### 2. **Technical & Coding**
- "Explain recursion to a 10-year-old using a real-world analogy"
- "Write a Python function that detects palindrome sentences, ignoring punctuation and case"
- "Design a simple REST API for a todo app with 3 endpoints"
- "Explain the difference between TCP and UDP using a pizza delivery analogy"
- "Write a regex pattern to validate email addresses with common edge cases"

### 3. **Philosophical & Conceptual**
- "If you could add one amendment to the US Constitution today, what would it be and why?"
- "Explain the concept of 'emergence' using three different real-world examples"
- "What does it mean for something to be 'true' in the age of AI-generated content?"
- "Is creativity combinatorial or truly novel? Argue both sides"
- "Define 'intelligence' in a way that includes both human and artificial forms"

### 4. **Practical & Problem-Solving**
- "Design a morning routine for maximum productivity and creativity"
- "Create a system for organizing digital files that balances simplicity with power"
- "How would you explain climate change to someone who doesn't believe in it?"
- "Design a beginner's guide to learning a new programming language in 30 days"
- "Create a decision-making framework for choosing between multiple job offers"

### 5. **Comparative Analysis**
- "Compare and contrast the writing styles of Hemingway and Faulkner"
- "What are the key differences between functional and object-oriented programming?"
- "Compare the leadership styles of Steve Jobs and Satya Nadella"
- "Explain the difference between machine learning and traditional programming"
- "Compare three different note-taking systems (Zettelkasten, Bullet Journal, Cornell)"

### 6. **Future & Speculative**
- "Describe what education might look like in 2050 with AI tutors"
- "What will be the most important ethical question for AI in the next decade?"
- "Design a city optimized for both human well-being and environmental sustainability"
- "How might social media evolve to be less addictive and more meaningful?"
- "What new forms of art might emerge from human-AI collaboration?"

### 7. **Meta & Self-Reflective**
- "What are the most common misconceptions about large language models?"
- "How can AI assistants like you be most helpful without being intrusive?"
- "What are the limitations of current AI that users should be aware of?"
- "How do different AI models approach the same problem differently?"
- "What makes a prompt 'good' for eliciting creative or insightful responses?"

## 📊 **Prompt Design Principles**

### **Good prompts should:**
- **Be specific but open-ended** - "Design a..." rather than "What is..."
- **Have multiple valid approaches** - No single right answer
- **Test different skills** - Creative, analytical, technical, etc.
- **Be interesting to compare** - Different models should produce meaningfully different responses
- **Have practical or educational value** - Results should be useful or insightful

### **Avoid:**
- **Factual recall questions** - "What year was the Declaration of Independence signed?"
- **Yes/no questions** - "Is AI dangerous?"
- **Vague prompts** - "Tell me something interesting"
- **Controversial topics** that might trigger safety filters excessively
- **Prompts that require real-time data** - "What's the weather in Tokyo right now?"

## 🎨 **Creative Constraints & Formats**

### **Add constraints for more interesting comparisons:**
- "In exactly 100 words..."
- "Using only food metaphors..."
- "As if explaining to a 5-year-old..."
- "In the style of a Shakespearean sonnet..."
- "As a series of tweets..."

### **Useful formats:**
- **Analogies**: "Explain X using Y analogy"
- **Design challenges**: "Design a system for..."
- **Comparative analysis**: "Compare and contrast X and Y"
- **Creative writing**: "Write a story about..."
- **Problem-solving**: "How would you solve X problem?"

## 🚀 **Quick Start Prompts**

### **For testing new models:**
```bash
mdls "Explain recursion using a Russian nesting doll analogy"
mdls "Write a Python function that generates the Fibonacci sequence"
mdls "Design a logo for a company called 'Quantum Bakes' that sells quantum physics-themed pastries"
```

### **For comparing model strengths:**
```bash
mdls "Explain the blockchain to a 10-year-old"  # Tests simplification
mdls "Write a sonnet about machine learning"    # Tests creative constraints
mdls "Design a better voting system"           # Tests practical innovation
```

## 📈 **Tracking & Improvement**

### **Keep a prompt journal:**
- Which prompts produced the most interesting comparisons?
- Which models excelled at which types of prompts?
- What prompt constraints led to the most creative responses?

### **Iterate based on results:**
- If a prompt produces similar responses across models, make it more specific
- If responses are too divergent, add more constraints
- Balance technical, creative, and analytical prompts
