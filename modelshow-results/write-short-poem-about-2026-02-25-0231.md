## 🏆 Winner: Claude Sonnet 4.5

**Three Points**

Three corners meet, three lines align,  
A shape both strong and sharp by design.  
From pyramids that touch the sky  
To sails that catch the wind up high.

Acute or obtuse, right-angled too,  
In every form, a different view.  
The strongest frame that builders know—  
Three sides that never bend or bow.

In mountains peaked and rooftops tall,  
In pizza slices, one and all,  
The humble triangle holds its ground:  
The simplest polygon around.

**Judge's Rating:** 9.5/10
**Why it won:** Claude delivers the best overall balance of poetic craft and substantive content. The three-stanza structure provides natural progression from form to function to examples, while maintaining consistent rhythm and rhyme throughout all 12 lines. The imagery is vivid and varied (pyramids, sails, pizza slices), the technical details are accurate (acute/obtuse/right angles), and lines like "Three sides that never bend or bow" combine structural truth with poetic flourish.
